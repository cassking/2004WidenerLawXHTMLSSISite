Office of Student Academic Affairs Delaware Campus

Wilmington, Delaware

Overview A new Office of Student Academic Affairs has been created to provide a comprehensive center for academic advising. Within the office, Professor Susan Goldberg will serve as Associate Dean for Student Academic Services; Professor Teresa Wallace will serve as Director of Student Academic Support; and, Professor Leslie Johnson will serve as Director of Student Life. In her capacity as Associate Dean for Student Academic Services, Professor Goldberg will coordinate the academic service for students and will work with the Vice Dean on scheduling and and academic instructional matters. Associate Dean Goldberg will also coordinate the provision of academic advising to second year students as well as accomodations for individuals with disabilities. As Director of Student Academic Support, Professor Wallace will develop an ongoing orientation program to supplement the initial orientation for incoming students. Professor Wallace will coordinate the provision of academic advising to first year students. In addition, she will continue to provide academic support to probationary students. As Director of Student Life, Professor Johnson will serve as liaison to the Student Bar Association and will supervise the activities of all student organizations. In addition, Professor Johnson will serve as advising coordinator for third year students. Appointments with the Associate Dean and Directors in the Office of Student Academic Affairs will continue to be made by calling Mrs. Donna Hoffecker at (302) 477-2023. The new office suite is located on the second floor of the Law Building, in the room 249-250 area. Services The Offices of the Office of Student Academic Affairs provide a variety of services to help students develop personally and professionally. Each campus has Staff Members who provide academic and personal counseling services to students and coordinate progress of general interest such as bar admission and the time management. The deans work closely with the Academic Support Program Directors on each campus to provide supplemental academic assistance to students. The Office oversees co-curricular organizations that are designed to develop students' legal skills such as the Law Review, Moot Court Honor Society and the Trial Advocacy Honor Society. The Deans also act as advisors to the Student Bar Association on each campus, the umbrella organizations for student service and support groups.
