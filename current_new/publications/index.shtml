Journals and Student Publications

Widener University School of Law currently publishes three printed student-run journals that offer students the opportunity to participate in and contribute to the exchange of high-quality legal scholarship:

  • Delaware Journal of Corporate Law
    Wilmington, Delaware
    was established in 1975 on the Wilmington Campus. The Journal publishes legal articles, notes and comments in the areas of corporate, business and banking law. It is published by a student editorial board and staff selected for their academic achievements, research skills and writing abilities. It is the only national law review that publishes unreported corporate opinions from the Delaware Court of Chancery. It has developed a reputation of excellence as a reference resource for the bench and bar.
  • The Widener Law Review
    Wilmington, Delaware
    was established in 1994 and is dedicated to exploring a different timely area of the law each year. The Widener Law Review is published by a student editorial board and staff selected for their academic achievements, research skills and writing ability and is based on a symposium held on the Wilmington Campus. Articles by symposium speakers form the core of the publication and student-authored pieces provide additional perspectives on the symposium topic. The first issue explored critical legal issues relating to the new health care economy
  • Widener Law Journal
    Harrisburg, Pennsylvania
    The Widener Law Journal is the official law review of the Widener University School of Law in Harrisburg. The Journal is entirely organized and managed by law students at the Harrisburg campus who have exhibited academic excellence and paramount legal writing abilities. Published three times per year, the Journal provides a vast forum for legal scholarship in all areas of the law. To achieve that end, the Journal welcomes the submission of unsolicited articles, essays, and commentaries authored by professors, attorneys, and others having scholarly legal insights. The Journal is committed to providing nothing short of academic excellence to the legal community while maintaining a close, working relationship with its authors, the practicing bar, and legal scholars nationwide.
