Employer Research Web Sites for Law Students

These sites should assist you in researching employers. They may help you find employers matching your skills and interests. They may also help you learn more about employers to which you know you want to apply or with which you will be interviewing soon.

  • Envirolink http://envirolink.netforchange.com/Links to environmental organizations' websites.
  • Essential Information http://www.essential.org/Links to public interest employers' Web Sites. Goodworks national directory of alternatives to corporate employment.
  • Idealist http://www.idealist.org/ Links to public interest organizations and resources. Career Center: database of public interest positions and internships (primarily non-legal), resume and cover letter writing for the public sector, salary surveys.
  • Lawinfo http://www.lawinfo.com/ Searchable database with law jobs for students and attorneys. Students and attorneys may post resumes.
  • National Association for Law Placement http://www.nalp.org/ Contains large firm hiring information, market trends and statistics.
  • Prosecuting and Government Attorneys http://www.co.eaton.mi.us/ecpa/proslist.htm Links to prosecuting attorneys, district attorneys, attorneys general and U.S. attorneys websites nationwide.
  • State Governments on The Web http://www.statelocalgov.net/index.cfm/ Extremely comprehensive site with links to individual state, multi-state, federal, national and other websites.
  • Yahoo's List of Law Firms Worldwide http://dir.yahoo.com Hundreds of links to law firms worldwide.
