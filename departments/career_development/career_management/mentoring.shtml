Mentoring Guide & Alumni Connections: an Overview

In 1999, the Career Development Office established the Widener Network Mentoring Program, which paired over 160 volunteer alumni, practitioners, and judges with law students during their first year of school. Many of the students met their mentors for the first time at the festive "Kick-Off" events held on each campus in October.

The program was established because many first-year law students are faced with the challenge of making major decisions early in their legal education, when they may know little about the legal profession. Mentors can provide the benefit of their knowledge and experience to help new students achieve their educational and professional goals.

Mentors gain a sense of accomplishment from having helped a future colleague and member of the Widener family. Students gain a valuable source of advice and guidance while navigating the rigors of the first year of law school.
