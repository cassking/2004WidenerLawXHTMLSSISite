Useful Job Listings/Job Search Web Sites for Law Students

Job-Search-Related Sites With Useful Links: The following sites allow students to access numerous other informative sites simply by clicking on the web address or area of interest to you.

  • Job site for the Law Student Division of the American Bar Association. http://www.abanet.org/
  • The Monster Board (provides links to hundreds of corporate employer profiles and lists many law-related positions) http://www.monster.com
  • Legal Forum (provides links to law firms, government agencies and bar associations nationwide) http://www.eaglelink.com/power/
  • The Harvard Public Interest Site (lists information about public interest opportunities and links to federal and state organizations as well as public interest groups) http://www.law.harvard.edu/Students/opia/ Researching or Locating Potential Employers
  • Martindale-Hubbell Lawyer Locator (contains law firm profiles and provides links to their web pages) http://www.martindale.com
  • Westlaw's Career Center http://www.lawschool.westlaw.com
  • National Association for Law Placement (NALP contains large firm hiring information as well as market trends and statistics) http://www.nalp.org
  • Yahoo's Business and Economy (contains law firm information) http://dir.yahoo.com/Business_and_Economy/Companies/Law/Firms
  • Findlaw (locates law firms, state government and other legal resources) http://www.findlaw.com
  • Infirmation More info on law firms, including inside scoop http://www.infirmation.com
  • Vault Reports (large law firms and corporate information http://www.vault.com
  • Leadership Directories (information on government agencies and officials, corporations, judges and law firms.) http://www.leadershipdirectories.com (contact the CDO for user name and password) Locating Specific Positions
  • Law Forum (Students can post resumes, browse http://www.lawforum.net
  • Duke's Listing of Federal Government Agency Deadlines for Summer Positions http://www.law.duke.edu/career/fedagency.html
  • ABA Net (lists internships and other legal resources) http://www.abanet.org
  • Legal Internships with federal agencies http://www.internships-usa.com (contact the CDO for user name and password)
