Widener's Grading Curve/Ranking Policy

Widener's grade curve

Our difficult grade curve is designed to ensure grading consistency and to avoid grade inflation. In first year classes, for example, professors must assign marks so that the average grade for the course is between a 2.5 and a 2.75 on a 4.0 scale. The grade curve for many upper level courses is only slightly more lenient. This rigorous grading system differentiates Widener from most other law schools and helps instill in students the kind of work ethic necessary to excel in today's legal marketplace. Employers are encouraged to keep these difficult grading standards in mind when evaluating applicants from Widener.

Widener's ranking policy

Our ranking policy is intended to provide employers with a meaningful basis for comparison. To help you better evaluate candidates from Widener, we publish the grade point average equivalents in 5% increments through the top 50% of each class, as well as the grade point average equivalents in thirds. Only the students in the top 10% of their class are assigned an individual numeric ranking.
