Student Housing: Delaware Campus

Housing is available to all Widener University School of Law students. The campus maintains two housing facilities, Concord Hall and Shipley Hall. There are 150 single occupancy, fully-furnished and air conditioned rooms between the two dormitories. Male and female students are housed separately either by floor or wing.

In addition the campus also has twenty-four furnished one and two bedroom townhouses. (All residents must be law students with the exception of students' spouses). Students in the townhouses are allowed to choose their own roommates.

The attractive campus is conveniently located and features well-lighted, safe parking areas and 24-hour security. A brochure describing the housing options and providing floor plans and costs is available upon request.

The university also provides telephone service to all residents. Pets and smoking are not permitted in any residents building.

In addition, many students elect off-campus housing in the many convenient and reasonably priced area apartment complexes. Information regarding nearby off-campus housing may be obtained from the Admissions Office. Within walking distance of campus are a large indoor shopping mall, supermarkets, hotels, banks, several places of worship and numerous restaurants.
