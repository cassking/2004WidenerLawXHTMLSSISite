The Trial Advocacy Institute: Certificate in Trial Advocacy Program

Wilmington, Delaware

Widener's Trial Advocacy Institute offers trial skills courses that follow the legal process from beginning to end: from initial client interview to summation before a jury. The pre-trial methods course teaches students how to gather and evaluate information, make discovery requests, prepare pre-trial motions and conduct settlement conferences. The trial methods course covers such areas as opening statements, closing arguments and examination of friendly and hostile witnesses. Widener also offers a wide variety of specialized courses and seminars that cover individual subjects in exhaustive detail and a unique, nationally recognized Intensive Trial Advocacy Program.

While all Widener students benefit from these basic advocacy courses, the Trial Advocacy Institute also offers students an opportunity to be awarded a Certificate in Trial Advocacy upon graduation. After completing the core advocacy courses, students develop more detailed litigation skills through advanced skills training courses and participation in a wide range of clinical programs. Participation in the Trial Advocacy Institute Certificate Program provides students with the tools necessary to become competent, professionally responsible trial advocates.
