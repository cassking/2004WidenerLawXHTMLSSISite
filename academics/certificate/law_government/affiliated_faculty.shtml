Law & Government Certificate Programs: Affiliated Faculty

Widener-Harrisburg's adjunct faculty are drawn from the large number of specialized state officials and private attorneys who work in the administrative law, constitutional law, legislation, and regulation areas. They bring substantial expertise and experience in advanced Law and Government subjects.

Professor James H. Cawley

Professor James H. Cawley teaches administrative law, appellate advocacy and public utility law. He is a private practitioner who specializes in public utility law. Professor Cawley served as majority counsel for the consumer affairs committee of the Pennsylvania Senate, as chief counsel for the Pennsylvania Senate Majority, and as a commissioner of the Pennsylvania Public Utilities Commission.

Professor G. Ronald Darlington

Professor G. Ronald Darlington teaches appellate practice and procedure. He is the executive administrator for the Commonwealth Court, which is a unique Pennsylvania court that hears all appeals from administrative agencies and government branches in the Commonwealth. He is co author of the treatise, PENNSYLVANIA APPELLATE PRACTICE.

Professor Robert H. Davis

Professor Robert H. Davis is a private practitioner who teaches legal ethics. He has served as Acting Chief Counsel and Deputy Chief Counsel to the Disciplinary Board of the Supreme Court of Pennsylvania, and Counsel to the West Virginia State Bar.

Professor Vincent C. Deliberato, Jr.

Professor Vincent C. Deliberato, Jr. teaches legislation and legislative drafting. He is a senior drafting attorney for the Pennsylvania Legislative Reference Bureau, and has served also served as a staff attorney for the Pennsylvania Code and Bulletin Section of the Bureau. He is a Pennsylvania commissioner to the National Conference of Commissioners on Uniform State Laws.
