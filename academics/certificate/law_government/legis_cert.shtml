Law & Government Certificate Programs: Law and Government Legislation Certificate Program

The National Conference of Commissioners on Uniform State Laws (NCCUSL) is a private nonprofit entity that creates proposed uniform or model statutes for the states. There is, however, a recurrent problem in this process. There are invariably conflicts between a proposed NCCUSL uniform or model statute and existing statutes and regulations in a state. NCCUSL cannot advise individual states on these conflicts, because the model statutes must be general; however, the statutes must be adopted or adapted to each individual state to eliminate conflicts with existing legislation. There is thus a problem with the NCCUSL process that the Law and Government Legislation fellows will solve.

This legislative specialty program will involve students in researching conflicts between an assigned proposed uniform or model statute and existing Pennsylvania statutes. After doing this research and analysis the student will explore modification by drafting proposed amendatory legislation, and/or suggesting abolition of existing statutes or statutory sections. The student will write a report about her research findings and proposals, and Widener Law School will print them. The school will then submit the written report to NCCUSL and the Pennsylvania legislature.

Requirements

  • Required/Elective Courses
  • Directed Research
  • Writing Requirement

Required Courses

In order to satisfy the required course segment for the Legislation Certification, a student must successfully complete the course in Legislation or Legislative Drafting.

Externship Requirement

In addition to the required course, in order to earn the Law and Government Legislation certificate, all students must successfully complete a three credit two semester sequence of directed research and writing [one (1) credit of directed research in the first semester and two (2) credits of directed research and writing in the second semester] under the direction of Professors Deliberato and Gedid, both of whom serve as NCCUSL commissioners for Pennsylvania.

Writing Requirement

In addition to the completion of the required course and directed research requirements, to earn the Law and Government Legislation certificate, a student must successfully complete a report of the research findings and recommendations relative to the model statute assigned to the student. This will be a substantial paper of at least twenty-five pages that will also satisfy the senior writing requirement.

The paper may be undertaken in one of three ways. It may be undertaken in conjunction with any seminar that satisfies the credit requirements for the specialty in environmental law. It may also be undertaken in conjunction with any course that satisfies the credit requirements for the specialty in environmental law, subject to the approval of the instructor of that course. Finally, it may be undertaken as a separate independent research course on an environmental law subject.
