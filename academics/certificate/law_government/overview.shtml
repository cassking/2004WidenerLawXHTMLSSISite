The Law and Government Institute's Law & Government Certificate Programs: Overview

What Is Government Law?

Government law, whether practiced with a private firm representing clients before an agency or government branch or as a lawyer representing the government, has grown exponentially in the past twenty to thirty years.

About The Programs

The Law and Government Certificate Program at Widener-Harrisburg has been designed to give J.D. candidates the opportunity to develop necessary skills for practice in this area through a curricular concentration. Located in Harrisburg, the capital of Pennsylvania, Widener offers a remarkable opportunity to study the operation of, and to conduct research in, law and government. Several faculty members with significant experience in this area pursue teaching, research and publication in law and government areas. Widener's location in the Pennsylvania capital, the center of government programs forone of the largest states, makes available to students numerous externships and other special programs with various agencies, Commonwealth Court, the legislature, the governor's counsel office and the attorney general. Widener's location also assures adjunct professors who are expert, specialized practitioners and counsel to the agencies, the legislature and private firms that practice before government. Students in the L&G Program also have the opportunity to have their work published in the Widener Law Journal, a scholarly journal devoted to the study of law and government, as well as significant opportunities for publication in other areas. Students who elect to pursue this certification graduate from law school with a firm foundation for practicing in the law and government area.

Many of the issues that deeply concern the public and our society involve how the government regulatesvarious areas, and how deeply the government should be involved in our lives. As our economy has grown, there has been a proliferation of agencies that deal with the public interest in such areas as economic regulation, safety and well-being of citizens, the operation of businesses in specialized areas, and benefit distribution. A listing of some of the areas in which there are government programs illustrates the growth in this area of the law: aging, banking, education, environmental regulation, government contracting, health care regulation, housing finance, human relations, insurance, labor, liquor, professional licensing, public utilities, securities, unemployment compensation, transportation, welfare, workplace safety and workers' compensation. Not only do lawyers play a crucial role in all of these areas, but the modern attorney must be an expert in administrative law and legislation in order to effectively represent his or her clients. Today's lawyer needs these specialized skills to work in this area as a private practitioner representing clients before the government or as a government lawyer.
