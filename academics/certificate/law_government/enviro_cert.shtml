Law & Government Certificate Programs: Environmental Law Certificate Program

The second type of specialty Law and Government certificate that a student may elect to earn is the Law and Government Environmental Law Certificate. STudents are eligible to receive the Law and Government Environmental Law certificate upon completing:

Requirements

  • Required/Elective Courses
  • Externship Requirement(modified for extended division students)
  • Writing Requirement

Required Courses

In order to satisfy the required course segment for certification, a student must successfully complete the course in State Administrative Law and four to six credits from the following courses:

  • Environmental Law (3 cr.)
  • International Environmental Law (2 or 3 cr.) (Also taught regularly in Widener international programs)
  • Land Use and Planning (2 cr.)
  • Seminars on Climate Change/Sustainability (2 cr. each) -- other environmental courses, when offered.

Externship Requirement

In addition to the required courses, in order to earn the Law and Government Environmental Law certificate, all Regular Division students must successfully complete one of the following externships:

  • Environmental Hearing Board
  • Game Commission
  • Pennsylvania Clean Air Council
  • Citizens for Pennsylvania's Future
  • Department of Environmental Protection Department of -Conservation and Natural Resources

Other externships will be considered to satisfy this requirement by the director of the program on a case-by-case basis as such other externships are brought to the attention of the program director.

Writing Requirement

In addition to the completion of the required courses and externship requirements, to earn the Law and Government Environmental Law certificate, a student must successfully complete a scholarly paper on an environmental law subject. This paper may be submitted to satisfy the upper class writing requirement that all students must meet. The Director of the Environmental Law Program must approve all paper topics submitted to satisfy this requirement in advance.

The paper may be undertaken in one of three ways. It may be undertaken in conjunction with any seminar that satisfies the credit requirements for the specialty in environmental law. It may also be undertaken in conjunction with any course that satisfies the credit requirements for the specialty in environmental law, subject to the approval of the instructor of that course. Finally, it may be undertaken as a separate independent research course on an environmental law subject.
