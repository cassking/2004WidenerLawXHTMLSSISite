The Law and Government Institute: Law & Government Certificate Programs

The Law and Government Institute is a part of Widener University School of Law-Harrisburg that makes available several curricular concentrations that students may elect to follow beginning in the first semester of their second year of studies (Regular Division students) or their third year of studies (Extended Division students). Students who successfully complete the program requirements will receive certification in Law & Government upon graduation.

Certificate Programs Available

  • General Law and Government Certificate Program
  • Law and Government Environmental Law Certificate Program
  • the Law and Government Legislation Certificate Program
  • A fourth Law and Government Institute certification program that will involve students in defending consumer interests in the legislative process will be available late in 2003. This will be one of the first programs of this type in the United States.

The object of this Law and Government Institute program is to increase the legal skills of students and, hence, employment opportunities; and to lift the level of expertise in the sector of the legal profession practicing law involving the government.

Director: Professor John Gedid

Requirements for Certification

In general, all of the Law and Government specialty programs involve three separate requirements that a student must satisfy in order to receive certification in that Law and Government specialty. Those necessary elements are:

  • Successful completion of required courses;
  • Successful completion of an externship (waived for extended division students who must take an elective course instead); and
  • Successful completion of the senior writing requirement on a Law and Government topic.
