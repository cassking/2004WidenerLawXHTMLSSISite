Law & Government Certificate Programs: Core Faculty

Professor John L. Gedid

Professor John L. Gedid is the director of the Law and Government Institute. He is the Official Reporter of Decisions for the Commonwealth Court, which hears all appeals from government agencies, commissions and branches. He recently served as chair of the Pennsylvania Bar Association Section on Administrative Law, and presently serves as editor of the Pennsylvania Bar Association Administrative Law Journal. He has published numerous articles on administrative law subjects. He is an elected member of the American Law Institute and a former member of the Pennsylvania Supreme Court Civil Procedure Rules Committee. Professor Gedid served as co-chair of the American Bar Association Task Force on Jurisdiction in Cyberspace-Sale of Goods. He also serves as a commissioner representing Pennsylvania to the National Conference of Commissioners on Uniform State Laws. He is currently writing a book on administrative law.

Vice Dean and Professor Robert C. Power

Vice Dean and Professor Robert C. Power has significant law and government experience as a trial attorney for the Regulatory Litigation Division of the Office of General Counsel of the U.S. Department of Energy, and as a Special Attorney in the Organized Crime and Racketeering Section, Criminal Division of the U.S. Department of Justice. He has also taught at Michigan State, Pace and Quinnipiac (formerly Bridgeport) Law Schools. Dean Power has published extensively in the areas of constitutional and administrative law, and is currently working on a book on the subject of legal ethics for government lawyers.

Professor John C. Dernbach

Professor John C. Dernbach served as director of the Advanced Science and Research Team of the Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Resources and as assistant counsel to the Bureau of Regulatory Counsel. He is co-author of a leading book on legal method and writing. He recently published a major new environmental law book on sustainable development. Professor Dernbach has published extensively in the area of environmental law and is currently focusing his attention on research and publication in the area of sustainable development. He is administers the L&G Environmental Law Program.

Professor Randy Lee

Professor Randy Lee is the chair of the Association of American Law Schools Section on Professional Responsibility. He has organized numerous symposia and published extensively in the area of legal ethics for government lawyers. He served on the Prison Legal Assistance Project Board of Directors and is serving on the Task Force on Deaf Rights to Mental Health Services for the American Deafness and Rehabilitation Association. Professor Lee also served as a Consultant for Deaf Rights for the Pennsylvania Office of Mental Health.
