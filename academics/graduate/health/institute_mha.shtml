Master of Health Administration (M.H.A.)

The Master of Health Administration (M.H.A.) is designed to give students the skills and knowledge essential to manage health care organizations. Students in Widener University's M.H.A. program gain knowledge about disease patterns, patients, clinicians, and treatment processes as well as accounting, economics, finance, marketing, strategic management, and other management skills. The program is especially appropriate for persons with a clinical background interested in preparing for or furthering their careers in the management and administration of hospitals, longterm care, health care systems, mental health, ambulatory care, and other health care facilities.

In designing the curriculum, the faculty has been responsive to the expressed needs of senior health care executives for wellprepared graduates to fill entry-level and middle management positions in health care organizations. The program is also designed for senior leadership roles in the health care field. This is accomplished by a sound curriculum that synthesizes management concepts and techniques applicable to any organization, and skills that can be specifically applied to health care organizations. The program provides a unique opportunity to gain valuable field experience through clerkship and management development projects. The program also offers opportunities for individual study and research. Offered in the evening, the program is designed especially for the part-time learner, the person who is currently employed but has not had graduate education in health care management.
