Master of Laws (LL.M.) in Health Law: Degree Requirements

Degree Requirements

Candidates for the degree of Master of Laws in Health Law are required to complete a total of 24 semester hours of coursework at the graduate level.

Twenty of the twenty-four credits needed for graduation must be in health law. The current health law courses along with their course descriptions are listed in this catalog. The remaining four elective credits may be in an unrelated subject area such as family law. Through the electives, students can individualize their course of study.

At least 18 of the total 24 semester hours must be completed in residence at Widener University School of Law.Ê Students may complete this residence requirement by attending as full-time or part-time students in the day or evening divisions.Ê Class schedules may be organized to accommodate the students' personal needs and LL.M. degree requirements.Ê Graduate students must complete all degree requirements within four years from the date of matriculation to qualify for the Master of Laws degree.

Academic Requirements

All graduate courses are graded A through F students may not take graduate courses on a pass/fail basis. Students may be required to retake a course in which they receive a grade of C- or lower. The Master of Laws degree is awarded only if the student has a cumulative grade point average of 2.5 or greater for (1) all 24 credits hours required to complete the degree, and (2) all credit hours in the general area of health law.Ê The same standards apply to international students, except that the relevant grade point average is 2.3.

A graduate student may be dismissed for poor scholarship at the end of any applicable grading period if he or she has a cumulative grade point average below that needed for the degree.Ê A graduate student having been so dismissed may apply for reinstatement under the applicable provision of the Widener University School of Law's Academic Code.

Transfer Credit

At the discretion of the Graduate Legal Studies Director, a candidate for the Master of Laws degree may be allowed to transfer credit for courses taken at another institution if: (1) the courses were taken at a law school accredited by the American Bar Association, (2) the courses were taken after the candidate completed his/her undergraduate law degree, (3) the courses were completed with a grade of C+ or better, (4) the candidate received no credit for these courses toward any other degree, and (5) the work was completed no earlier than three years prior to the date of matriculation for the graduate degree at Widener University School of Law.

No more than six hours of transfer credit will be counted toward the Master of Laws degree. Transfer grades will not be counted in computing the student's cumulative grade point average.
