Master of Jurisprudence (M.J.) in Health Law: Admissions & Financial Aid

Admissions

Applicants must have at least a bachelor's degree, and preferably will have recent or current professional employment in the health, pharmaceutical, or biotechnology industries.

Applicants do not need to take any graduate school admission exams, such as the GMAT or LSAT, for admission to the M.J. program.

Applications should be received on or before May 20.

Applicants are considered for admission when the Graduate Programs Office has received a completed application form, official undergraduate transcripts from all colleges and universities attended, a resume, a personal statement, two letters of recommendation, and the $60.00 non-refundable application fee payable to: Widener University.

Application materials should be mailed to:

Graduate Programs Office
Widener University School of Law
4601 Concord Pike
P.O. Box 7474
Wilmington, DE 19803-0474

Applications should be received on or before July 1st for admission to the fall semester and on or before November 1st for the spring semester.

Financial Aid

Students should apply for financial assistance at the same time they apply for admission to the Master of Jurisprudence program. Applications for aid are not reviewed until applicants have been admitted to the Master of Jurisprudence program. Students are eligible to apply for the Federal Stafford Loan Program.

Much of the School of Law's financial assistance is supported by the United States government and is generally not available for citizens of other nations. For application forms and complete information concerning financial aid, please contact:
