Health Law Institute: Facilities & Resources

Law Library/Legal Information Center

The Widener University School of Law Legal Information Center houses one of the most significant legal collections in the region. The collection exceeds 400,000 volumes on the Delaware Campus. The Information Center also provides connectivity to online databases, the Internet and CD-ROM products.

The focus on our services, collections, and use of advanced technologies represents a commitment to develop and maintain an excellent research facility. A knowledgeable reference staff is always prepared to assist students in legal research technologies.

The Delaware Campus Information Center has an outstanding collection in health law, bioethics, toxic torts, and comparative health law, as well as corporate law, finance, international business and taxation. The Information Center also houses a comprehensive collection of administrative agency decisions, legal periodicals, a wide range of loose-leaf services and a sizable microform collection. The Information Center is designated as a selective depository for United States government documents. In addition, the library's treatise collection is continually expanding to reflect new and developing areas of the law.

Our resources allow students to conduct research using the latest in computer technology. Computers provide access to legal and interdisciplinary research, email, computer-assisted legal instruction, word processing and other applications. All students are issued personal passwords which provide unlimited access to Lexis and Westlaw legal research databases. Numerous legal and nonlegal CD-ROM databases are available from networked and dedicated workstations. Computers located throughout the Information Center are used for legal research and for the preparation of legal briefs, memoranda, and law review articles. Students and faculty alike are afforded the resources of a high quality Legal Information Center that strives to keep pace with current technologies in the legal information field.

Career Development Office

The central focus of the Career Development Office is to help students formulate and realize educational and professional goals. The office maintains a complete reference library that includes legal directories and manuals on topics such as the establishment of a solo practitioner's office. On- and off-campus interviews are arranged through this office.

The office also maintains an extensive file of job listings available nationally within law and accounting firms, corporations, government agencies, and public interest organizations. Graduate students are encouraged to contact the Career Development Office for assistance with goal setting, career research and the job application process. In addition, the director of Graduate Legal Studies and the members of the graduate faculty are available to counsel students and to help graduates secure meaningful specialized employment.
