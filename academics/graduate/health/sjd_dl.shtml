Doctoral Programs (S.J.D. / D.L.) in Health Law

Doctor of Juridical Science (S.J.D.)

This doctoral program is designed for attorneys with a strong scholarly interest in health care law and policy. By facilitating a focused scholarly effort on a specific health law topic, this degree will further enhance the recipient's health law expertise and allow the recipient to produce an academic work of publishable quality. Applicants must hold a J.D. and either an LL.M. in Health Law or a related field, or a Master's in Health Administration or Public Health.

Doctor of Laws (D.L.)

This doctoral program is designed for graduates of a Master of Jurisprudence (M.J.) or a Master of Juridical Science (M.S.J.) in Health Law program. It requires students to engage in in-depth research and complete a substantial written scholarly work, thereby enhancing their expertise in health law and policy.
