Master of Laws (LL.M.) in Health Law

The fundamental objective of a Master of Laws (LL.M.) in Health Law is to offer advanced training in health law. Its goal is to prepare lawyers for advanced health law counseling, litigation, and transactional work by offering sequences of courses taught by excellent academics and experienced practitioners. The School of Law is renowned for the excellence of its corporate and finance offerings; the Health Law LL.M. draws on these strengths, adding a substantial regulatory and bioethical focus to the study of health law.
