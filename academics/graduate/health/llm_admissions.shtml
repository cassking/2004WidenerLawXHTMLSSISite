Master of Laws (LL.M.) in Health Law: Admissions & Financial Aid

Admissions

Candidates for the Master of Laws degree in Health Law who are American citizens must have graduated from a law school that has been approved by the American Bar Association.Ê Although domestic applicants are generally expected to have graduated in the top one-third of their J.D. class, no specific grade point average or class standing is required.Ê Candidates with strong employment or other relevant background are encouraged to apply.Ê Similarly, outstanding graduates of foreign law schools will be accepted if their educational backgrounds indicate a strong likelihood of success in the program.

Applicants must complete the application form and enclose a personal statement, resume, and the application fee. Applicants must ensure that each college and university attended (undergraduate and graduate) sends the Graduate Programs Office an official transcript detailing the academic record of the applicant, including degrees earned and dates awarded. In addition, one letter of recommendation is required, and should be provided with the application or sent directly to the Graduate Programs Office. The non-refundable $60.00 application fee must accompany all forms, and the check or money order should be payable to Widener University.

Foreign law graduates are also required to submit an official report of their score on the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL). Widener's institution code is 2642. Additional information regarding requirements for international applicants may be obtained by contacting the Graduate Programs Office.

Applications from United States citizens should be received by the Graduate Programs Office by August 1 for admission to the fall semester, and by December 1 for admission to the spring semester. Applications from non-U.S. citizens should be received by the Graduate Programs Office before June 1 for the fall semester, and by November 1 for admission to the spring semester. (Note: foreign trained lawyers usually are not admitted to begin in the spring semester.)

Financial Aid

Students should apply for financial assistance at the same time they apply for admission to the Master of Laws program. Applications for aid are not reviewed until applicants have been admitted to the Master of Laws program. Financial assistance can consist of a grant, loan, or both. There is also a limited opportunity for part-time employment on a per-hour basis in the library or as a work-study/research assistant.

Much of the School of Law's financial assistance is supported by the United States government and is generally not available for citizens of other nations. For application forms and complete information, please contact:

Widener University School of Law
Financial Aid Office
P.O. Box 7474
Wilmington, DE 19803-0474

International law graduates will be expected to provide verification of financial responsibility prior to Widener's forwarding appropriate documentation to U.S. Immigration officials as the basis for issuance of the student visa to enter this country.
