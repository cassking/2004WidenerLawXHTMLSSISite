Doctoral Programs (S.J.D. / D.L.) in Health Law: Curriculum

Individualized programs will be designed for the S.J.D. and D.L. candidates. During the first year of study, the doctoral students will be required to take courses designed to enhance and expand their research skills in preparation for writing their dissertation. Doctoral students may complete some advanced health law courses depending on the experience of the student. Every doctoral candidate will be required to enroll in an Advanced Research Methods Course each semester. In addition to course work, doctoral candidates may perform some fieldwork, such as lectures, course development or continuing education instruction. During the second year, the candidates will write a doctoral dissertation and present it to the doctoral committee and health law community. Dissertations will be supervised by a member of the core health law faculty and will be a subject within the expertise of the core health law faculty. Such areas of expertise include:

  • Health care liability
  • Managed care legal issues
  • Food and drug law
  • Health care antitrust
  • Bioethics, including reproductive technology and issues in death and dying
  • HIV/AIDS
  • Law and science
  • Toxic torts
  • Health care regulation and finance
