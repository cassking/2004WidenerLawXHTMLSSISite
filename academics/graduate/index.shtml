Graduate Programs at Widener Law

Widener has a national reputation for graduate programs in corporate and health law, providing a sophisticated curriculum for advanced professionals. Students come from across the country and around the world because Widener offers a wider range of electives than most American law schools. The experience is further enriched through contact with other students of similar interests and backgrounds.

Graduate Programs in Corporate Law

  • General Information
  • Master of Laws in Corporate Law and Finance (LL.M.): This program is designed to furnish the highly qualified student with the knowledge and analytical skills needed for success in our increasingly globalized economy.

Graduate Programs in Health Law

  • General Information
  • Master of Laws in Health Law (LL.M.) This program prepares lawyers for careers in advanced health law counseling, litigation and transactional work.
  • Master of Jurisprudence in Health Law (M.J.) This program is designed for health care and other professionals who desire to expand their education and gain a broadened understanding of health care's legal and policy environment.
  • Doctor of Juridical Science (S.J.D.) By facilitating a focused scholarly effort on a specific health law topic, this degree further enhances an attorney's health law expertise through the production of an academic work of publishable quality.
  • Doctor of Laws (D.L.) This doctoral program is designed for graduates with a Health Law M.J. or M.S.J.
  • Juris Doctor/Master of Library Science (J.D./M.S.L.S.) Widener's Harrisburg Campus, in cooperation with Clarion University of Pennsylvania, offers a Juris Doctor/Master of Library Science (J.D./M.S.L.S.) that integrates legal training and the principles and techniques of library science with applications of information technology.
