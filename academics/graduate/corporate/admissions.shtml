Master of Laws (LL.M.) in Corporate Law and Finance: Admissions, Eligibility & Deadlines

Eligibility

Candidates for the Master of Laws in Corporate Law and Finance must have graduated from law school, whether a United States law school accredited by the American Bar Association, or a similarly recognized institution abroad. Although graduates of ABA approved law schools are expected to have done well in law school, no specific grade point average or class rank is required for admission. Candidates with strong employment or other relevant background are encouraged to apply. Similarly, graduates of foreign law schools will be accepted if their educational and/or professional backgrounds indicate a strong likelihood of success in the program.

Admission

Applicants for admission must complete the application form and enclose a personal statement, resume, and the application fee. Applicants must request each college attended (undergraduate and graduate) to send to the Graduate Programs Office an official transcript detailing the academic record of the applicant, including degrees earned and dates awarded. In addition, one letter of recommendation is required, and should be provided with the application or sent directly to the Graduate Programs Office. The non-refundable $60 application fee must accompany all forms. Check or money order should be made payable to Widener University.

The application form provided must be completed in full and signed by the applicant. It should be mailed to:

Graduate Programs Office
Widener University School of Law
4601 Concord Pike
P.O. Box 7474
Wilmington, DE 19803-0474

Foreign law graduates seeking admission are also required to submit an official report of their score on the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL). Widener's Institution code is 2642. Non U.S. Citizens must also provide an affadavit of support and an original bank statement showing sufficient resources to cover tuition and living expenses.

Deadlines

Applications from United States citizens should be received by the Graduate Programs Office on or before August 1 for admission to the fall semester and on or before December 1 for admission to the spring semester.

Applications from non-U.S. citizens should be recieved by the Graduate Programs Office on or before May 1 for the fall semester.

The application and all materials become the property of the Law School and are not returnable.
