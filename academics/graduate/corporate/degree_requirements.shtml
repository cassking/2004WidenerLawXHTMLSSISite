Master of Laws (LL.M.) in Corporate Law and Finance: Degree Requirements

Candidates for the degree of Master of Laws in Corporate Law and Finance are required to complete a total of 24 semester hours of coursework at the graduate level, including at least 20 in corporate law, international law, taxation, or finance. While most students complete all of their course work in Wilmington, graduate credit may also be earned through enrollment in the School of Law summer programs at The Graduate Institute of International Studies in Geneva, Switzerland, the University of Nairobi in Kenya, and Macquarie University in Sydney, Australia. Applicants interested in obtaining information about these programs should so indicate on their application.

At least 18 of the total 24 semester hours must be completed in residence at Widener University School of Law. Students may complete this residence requirement by attending as full-time or part-time students in the day or evening. Class schedules may be organized to accommodate the student's personal needs and Master of Laws degree requirements. Graudate students must complete all degree requirements within four years from the date of matriculation to qualify for the Master of Laws degree.

Graduate courses in corporate law and finance carry between two and four semester hours of credit. Normally graduate credit is not given to students from the United States for the basic course in Business Organizations, which is usually completed at the Juris Doctor level. We recommend that this course be completed before beginning graduate work.

Required Courses for Graduates of ABA Approved Law Schools

  • Colloquium (2 credits)
  • Corporate Finance (2 or 3 credits)
  • Securities Regulation (3 credits)
  • Seminar: Advanced Corporations (2 credits)

Required Courses for International Students

  • Business Organizations (4 credits)
  • Colloquium (2 credits)
  • Corporate Finance (2 or 3 credits)
  • Introduction to United States Law and Legal Institutions (3 credits)
  • Securities Regulation (3 credits)
