Master of Laws (LL.M.) in Corporate Law and Finance

Delaware may be small state, but it is enormous in the world of corporate law. The state's status as the unofficial "corporate law capital" derives from the fact that hundreds of thousands of companies have incorporated in Delaware and have their internal affairs governed by Delaware corporate law. The large number of companies regulated by Delaware has effectively made Delaware's corporate law the corporate law for much of the United States and the world. Lawyers who counsel clients on corporate matters must thus become familiar with Delaware's corporate code and the jurisprudence interpreting it. Widener's Corporate Law and Finance LL.M. provides an ideal means to obtain that expertise.
