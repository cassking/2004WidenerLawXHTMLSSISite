Delaware Bar Readiness Program: Sessions & Attendance

What should I do if ...

Answers to common questions:

... my schedule will not allow me to attend all the sessions?

It is recommended that you attend all lectures included in the program so that you maximize the benefits available from this opportunity. If you are not able to, you should attend as many as possible.

Participants planning to submit practice essays for scoring and feedback should attend, at a minimum, all Barbri BEAT Day lectures. The testing units of the program are designed such that essay topics are derived primarily from Barbri lecture coverage. Widener faculty presentations will either supplement this coverage or provide you an opportunity to explore other subject matter related topics appearing on the bar examination.

All lectures will be videotaped to allow later viewing by those unable to attend a scheduled lecture. Lectures presented by law school faculty will be available for overnight checkout while Barbri presentations may only be viewed in house.

... I am unable to attend the in-class testing opportunities?

An examination drop-off collection box will be located in the legal information center for participants who are unable to attend the Thursday in-class testing sessions. These students will be permitted to pick up essay materials from the Bar Programs Office earlier in the testing week for submission by the drop-off deadline (11 p.m.). Late submissions will not be scored.
