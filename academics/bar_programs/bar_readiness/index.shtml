Delaware Bar Readiness Program: Clearing The Hurdles

What is the Widener Bar Readiness Program?

The WBRP is a spring bar lecture and testing program, presented by law school faculty and Barbri of PA/NJ, covering the six multistate bar examination subjects and other bar related topics, and includes five practice test opportunities. This years program consists of substantive lectures covering: civil procedure (Fed. & Pa.), constitutional law, contracts, criminal law/procedure, evidence, real property, sales, corporations, and torts. In addition, lectures will cover strategies for writing essay answers and the MPT. Dona Zimmerman, a recognized expert in multistate bar examination testing and Widener law alumna, will also present a free PMBR mini-review and strategy session.

This course is intended to supplement rather than replace a more comprehensive commercial review course you can take closer to the actual bar examination dates. It is strongly recommended that you attend all lectures or as many as your schedule will permit. In selecting events to attend, remember to consider your performance in relevant required and elective course work.

Bar Review Readiness Schedules are posted on this site at the beginning of each school year. They are offered for download in MS Word and PDF format. (What is a PDF?) These documents may be updated during the year. (Look for the most recent "Last Updated" label.)

Who can participate?

The program is designed primarily for students and alumni planning to sit for a bar examination in 2004. However, other upper level students will be welcomed to attend.
