Delaware Bar Review Program: Tuition & Fees

Tuition for first-time Widener Bar Review takers is $1,900.

If tuition is paid in full by May 3, 2004, then a $100 discount is available. Tuition for Widener Bar Review Alumni is $750. This tuition is also eligible for the $100 discount if paid by May 3, 2004.

Credit for Bar/Bri deposits will be honored upon receipt of required documentation. Students should contact Bar/Bri directly to obtain required verification.

Late Fee

A $100 late fee will be imposed on registrations received after May 19, 2004.

Refunds

Refunds, less the $100 non-refundable registration fee, will be issued prior to the first day of classes. After the first day of classes, refunds will be pro-rated based on the number of classes held and when materials have been returned. Students must notify the Bar Programs Office in writing immediately of their withdrawal. No refunds will be issued after June 1, 2004. No refunds will be issued for used materials. All Bar/Bri and Delaware state materials must be returned to obtain a refund.

Early Bird Special

Students can get a head start studying for the bar exam by attending multistate lectures on four of the six multistate subjects tested on the Multistate Bar Examination (MBE). These lectures are open to students who have paid $500 toward their course tuition by March 19, 2004. All attendees will also receive their 2004 Bar/Bri Multistate Outlines Book at the first lecture. Additional Information

  • On-Campus Housing: On-campus housing will be available for any interested Bar Review student for the duration of the Bar Review program and Bar Exam. Please contact our office if you are interested in receiving information and an application.
  • Parking: Free parking at the law school is available to all course participants.
  • Americans with Disabilities Act: If you are a person with a disability and want to request an accommodation under the Americans with Disabilities Act, please contact the Bar Programs Office for a "Request For Accommodation" form. Request must be made at least 90 days before the start of the course to ensure timely receipt.
