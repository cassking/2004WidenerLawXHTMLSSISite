Summer Institutes at Widener Law

Overview

Studying overseas can be a tremendous asset to a legal education. Four summer Summer Abroad Law Institutes - Nairobi, Kenya; Venice, Italy; Geneva, Switzerland; and Sydney, Australia - offered by Widener University School of Law enable you to explore different cultures and the perspectives of students and faculty in foreign countries. All Summer Abroad Law Institutes are coordinated by the International Programs Office.

As the world economy has become truly international, an understanding of global business, trade and jurisdiction has become essential for the modern lawyer. These institutes offer a variety of courses and educational opportunities often unavailable in the United States. In addition, internships with international organizations may be available to students in these programs. Interested students should pursue this possibility with the director of the specific program to which they are applying.

In summer 2003, Widener University School of Law conducted programs in Geneva, Switzerland; Venice, Italy; and Sydney, Australia. In Geneva, 12 students attended, 8 from Widener, and 5 from schools other than Widener, with 5 different law schools from throughout the United States represented. In Venice, Italy, 27 students attended the institute, with 16 students from Widener, 9 from schools other than Widener; overall, 9 different law schools from the United states were represented. In Sydney, Australia, 32 students attended; 8 from Widener, 24 from schools other than Widener, with 24 different law schools from throughout the United States represented.

Information for Applicants

All of our programs are approved by the American Bar Association. Students are permitted to take up to two courses totaling six credits (or, alternately, one course for three credits). For the 2004 programs, applications will be accepted until Thursday, April 15, 2004.

Applications accepted after the deadline will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis. Admissions for each program are done on a rolling basis, with acceptance into any one of the institutes normally occurring within two weeks of application.

Applications for the 2004 programs are also available on line. Students are encouraged to Apply Online and to send a letter of good standing as soon as possible after the on line application is submitted to:

Important note: the criteria for acceptance into any one of the four programs Widener Law offers is the following:

Acceptance Criteria

  • Students must have completed their first full year of law studies. For extended-division students "first full year" is defined as approximately 32 - 33 credits (usually taking 3 semesters to earn), which is comprised of required first year law courses at any ABA-approved law school.
  • Students must provide a letter of good standing from their home law school at time of application and a tuition deposit of $50 which is refundable if the applicant is refused admission. Applications for all programs are due no later than April 15, 2004. Students who participated in an institute and return to the same destination the following year, qualify for half-price tuition.
