Geneva, Switzerland Summer Abroad Law Institute: Courses

Comparative Criminal Procedure

(Professor Diehm, faculty) This course will compare the common law system of criminal justice of the United States and England with the inquisitorial system found in Western Europe and many other countries. We will begin with an examination of the historical roots of each system and then proceed to a review of the procedures of the common law system. Thereafter, we will study the Continental Inquisitorial system and how it functions in France and Germany. Finally, we will discuss a German homicide trial and the relative merits of the two systems of criminal justice. (3 credits)

International Criminal Law

(Professor Sosnov, faculty) This course will cover selected topics involving crimes and criminals beyond the borders of the United States. We will examine transactional prosecutions, the extraterritorial application of United States criminal laws. We will also explore the legal limits on attempts by United States officials to interrogate and otherwise obtain evidence from individuals outside of the United States. Another major focus will be on crimes in violation of international law, and international tribunals, such as the Nuremberg Tribunal and the current International Criminal Court, which involve prosecutions for genocide, crimes against humanity and war crimes. (3 credits)

International Human Rights

(Professor Jimenez, faculty) This course capitalizes on Geneva as home to the major United Nations international human rights bodies. We will discuss the development of international human rights law within the framework of public international law, the issues surrounding it and how it applies to national jurisdictions. We will analyze the role of international human rights mechanisms in the protection of individuals and groups and obtain an overview of regional systems. We will study selected treaties and examine their application. Lastly, we will look into the interface of humanitarian law, refugee law, criminal law, and anti-terrorism legislation with human rights. (3 credits)
