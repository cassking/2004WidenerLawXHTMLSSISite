Geneva, Switzerland Summer Abroad Law Institute

Widener University School of Law's summer International Law Institute in Geneva, entering its thirteenth year in 2004, offers students a unique experience. Since the founding of the United Nations in Geneva in 1920, the city has been known throughout the world as an international center for peace and diplomacy. Geneva is home to numerous international organizations, such as the International Red Cross, the United Nations European Headquarters, the World Health Organization (WHO), and the World Trade Organization (WTO).

In cooperation with the Graduate Institute of International Studies (HEI), the Geneva International Law Institute takes advantage of its city's unique resources by offering three courses of three credits each: Comparative Criminal Procedure, International Criminal Law, and International Human Rights. Classes are held four or five days per week, schedule dependent, from June 4 to July 11, 2004 (more specific information concerning the actual weekly schedule will be available in late Fall 2003). Geneva is a lovely city located on Lake Geneva, and a city that is a pleasure to explore on foot. It is conveniently located for easy travel by train throughout Europe. Students may hike in the Jura Mountains and the Alps and rafting and mountain biking trips are also available.
