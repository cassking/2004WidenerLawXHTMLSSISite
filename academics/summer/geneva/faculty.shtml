Geneva, Switzerland Summer Abroad Law Institute: Faculty

James W. Diehm

James W. Diehm, Director, Geneva International Law Institute, 2004; B.A., The Pennsylvania State University; J.D. Georgetown University Law Center; Professor of Law, Widener University School of Law. Before coming to the law school, Professor Diehm served as the Presidentially Appointed United States Attorney for the District of the Virgin Islands, and prior to that, served as an Assistant Attorney General for the Virgin Islands and an Assistant United States Attorney for the District of Columbia. In the fall of 1997, Professor Diehm was sent as a legal specialist by the United States Department of Justice and the American Bar Association on a three-month mission to the Ukraine and Russia to start the Criminal Law Reform Project in the Ukraine.

Leonard Sosnov

Leonard Sosnov, B.S., Temple University; J.D., Harvard; Professor of Law, Widener University School of Law. Former Chief of Law Reform, Defender Association of Philadelphia. Extensive criminal trial and appellate experience, including briefing and arguing two cases before the United States Supreme Court.

Cecilia Jimenez

Cecilia Jimenez, LL.M, Pubic International Law, King’s College London; LLB, Ateneo de Manila University. Cecilia Jimenez is a Philippine Lawyer who is currently an international human rights consultant and trainer. She has received training and research mandates from, among others, the U.N. Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights, the OSCE, the Conference of NGOs in Consultative Status with the U.N., the Swiss Department of Foreign Affairs, and international and national NGOs. She has given human rights training and lectures for lawyers, U.N. military observers, international civilian peacekeepers, minority groups, NGOs and students of law and international relations. She has taught the Widener International Human Rights law course since 2002. She also writes on international legal developments in human rights and on the U.N. Previously, she worked with the Philippine NGOs and coordinated the Asian caucus during the 1993 World Conference on Human Rights, had served in advisory capacities to Amnesty International and the FIDH, and was U.N. program director of the Association for the Prevention of Torture in Geneva.
