Venice, Italy Summer Abroad Law Institute: Courses

Comparative Health Law

(Professor Furrow faculty) This course examines the ways in which European countries (with particular attention to Italy) finance health care; control the rising costs of their health care systems; promote quality care; and resolve the ethical problems faced by patients and health care professionals, with attention to end-of-life ethical issues. Continental approaches are compared to the American system.
(3 credits)

International Business Transactions

(Professor Marrella faculty) This course will look at the process by which international business is conducted across national boundaries, how disputes are resolved, and the role of the lawyer in facilitating such transactions. Topics may include the law relevant to private transactions by which individuals and nations import and export goods, transfer technology, or make direct foreign investments. The domestic law of European nations and the United States - and how such laws affect international transactions - will also be explored. The course will also consider the impact of international organizations on private transactions, and the role of international arbitration in resolving disputes.
(3 credits)

Comparative Sales Law

(Professor Moringiello faculty) An international sales law course that will cover the United Nation’s convention on contracts for the international sale of goods (CISG), payments by letter of credit, and, possibly, dispute resolution issues such as choice of law and choice of forum.
(3 credits)
