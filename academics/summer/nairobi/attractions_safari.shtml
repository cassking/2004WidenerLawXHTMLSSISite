Nairobi, Kenya Summer Abroad Law Institute: On Safari

This year we have planned three group safaris at steeply discounted prices to enhance your stay in Kenya. The weekend before the program begins (June 12-13) we will travel to the Lake Naivaisha in the Great Rift Valley, the mammoth fissure in the earth's surface where the great anthropological discoveries have been made. This freshwater lake has an abundant hippo population and over 300 species of birdlife. We will take a launch to the Crescent Island Game Sanctuary where we walk among giraffe, zebras, gazelles, and other non-predatory wildlife. There will be an opportunity to climb the extinct volcano, Mt. Longonot that rises to 9000 feet above the Rift Valley floor. For more information on Mt. Longonot, consult the following website: http://www.kilimanjaro.com

After the first week of classes (June 18-20) we will journey to the Samburu National Reserve on a three-day safari adventure. We will drive around the 17,000 feet jagged peak of Mt. Kenya and then down into the subdesert region of Samburu. The Ewaso Nyiro River runs through the Park and attracts abundant wildlife. You will encounter leopard, cheetah, elephant, ostrich, gerenuk antelope, crocodile, Grevys zebra, and the reticulated giraffe.

After exams on Friday morning, July 30, you will depart in minivans on a three day, two night safari to the world renown Maasai Mara Game Reserve. It is a four hour drive down into the Great Rift Valley and then over dirt roads to the park. The Mara is the finest park in Kenya and the venue of the annual migration of wildebeest and zebra. Perhaps you will see the migration as we have in the past, as well as lion, cheetah, giraffe, elephant, and leopard.

In the past we have stayed at the luxurious five star Mara Simba lodge on the Telek river at special rates. It is one of the newer lodges at the Mara and has excellent modern facilities including a shower, toilet, and a private veranda facing over the river. There is a guest dining room, lounge, bar, and a swimming pool. The cost will be about $210 per person in a shared room. This price includes all transportation, including a guide and game drives, meals, lodging, and student park fees.

There will be plenty of time on our three-day weekends and on the week off (July 4-11) to visit the other parks and sights in Kenya and Tanzania. In the past students have used this time to climb Mt. Kilimanjaro, visit the bird sanctuaries in the Great Rift Valley such as Lake Nakuru National Park (http://www.kws.org/nakuru.htm) and Lake Baringo, relax on Indian Ocean beaches and enjoy the several underwater marine parks along the coral reef, visit the mountain gorillas in Uganda, or travel to the exotic islands of Zanzibar and Lamu.
