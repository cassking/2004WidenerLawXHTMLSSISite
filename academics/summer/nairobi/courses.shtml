Nairobi, Kenya Summer Abroad Law Institute: Courses

Comparative Constitutional Law

(Professors Kibwana and Gathii faculty )


This course examines and compares the structure and historical background of the constitutional systems in Kenya and the United States, including an examination of core issues such as the separation of powers, the role and independence of the judiciary, and guaranteed human rights and liberties.

(3 credits) (9 a.m. - 10:50 a.m.)

International Environmental Law and Trade

(Professors Kelly and Mumma faculty )


This course examines the different philosophical premises and legal structures of these two important areas of international law and their intersection. We will study the legal regimes to alleviate the global problems of vanishing biodiversity, greenhouse gases, and the ozone layer and conservation issues crucial to Kenya. We will also examine the World Trade Organization and its rules and decisions relevant to the environment. There will be guests lecturers from international organizations.

(3 credits) (11:00 a.m.-12:50p.m.)

Comparative Family Law

(Professors Muigai and Kameri-Mbote faculty )


This course examines the complex issues of Kenyan and U.S. family law. Students will be introduced to the various systems of law that apply in Kenya including Tribal, Christian, and Islamic law. The course examines the different modes of social control and the assumptions of social organization that underpin each system.

(3 credits) (2:00-3:50 p.m.)
