Nairobi, Kenya Summer Abroad Law Institute: Air Travel & Visas

While students are responsible for making their own travel arrangements to and from Nairobi, we will provide you with information on steeply discounted round trip airfares to Nairobi many with stopovers in Europe. Many students visit London, Paris, Amsterdam or Rome before or after the program.

Low-cost airfares are available from special student discount travel agencies for approximately $1200 to $1500 round trip depending upon the city of your departure. Do not use your local travel agent or the airlines if you want the best fares.

The best fares are generally available through Magical Holidays, a consolidator specializing in travel to Africa. Call them at (800) 228-2208. Two student travel agencies, Council Travel, at http://www.counciltravel.com and STA Travel at www.sta-travel.com offer special fares and health insurance as part of membership in the Council on the International Educational Exchange. Further information will be available once the fares are determined for next summer.

Visas are required. Our program director will send you a visa application after you send in your application. You will receive a tourist visa. You can also obtain a visa from the Kenyan Embassy in Washington, D.C. at (202) 387-6101 or on the internet at http://kenyaembassy.com. Ask for a tourist visa application. Do not ask for a student visa because you stay will be a short one and you will not be a full time student at the University of Nairobi. Further information on visas, housing, airfares, and health requirements can be obtained from director, Professor Patrick Kelly at (302) 477-2124 or j.p.kelly@law.widener.edu.
