Nairobi, Kenya Summer Abroad Law Institute: The Indian Ocean & Beaches

Kenya is blessed with an abundance of palm-fringed sugar beaches and a coral reef that extend the entire length of the Indian Ocean coast. The beaches north and south of Mombasa are a train, plane or bus ride away from Nairobi. There is a full range of international hotels right on the beach at reasonable prices. Many have rooms available in June and July at $15-$30 per person per night including three meals. There are also beach houses for rent. Several members took a scuba course and dived or snorkeled among the undersea wildlife.

North of Mombasa there are more beaches at Watamu and Malindi, Arabic ruins, and the big game fishing haunts of Ernest Hemingway. Just off the northern coast lies the unspoiled paradise of Lamu Island. You will not find a more relaxing setting than this stronghold of Swahili culture. For several years members have fallen in love with the nearby pristine island of Zanzibar for its beautiful beaches and excellent diving. Others have journeyed to the Indian Ocean islands of Madagascar, Mauritius, and the Seychelles. There are far more things to do in and around Kenya than you will have time to explore.
