  • How is my application processed?/ When should I expect to be notified that I've been accepted to the program?

    Answer Six:     Applications are sent to the International Programs Coordinator. The applicant's administrative information is entered into Widener Law School's database, and application fees are deposited with Widener Law School's Bursar's Office.

    All application materials are then forwarded to the specific program director, and a letter of acceptance should be regular mailed or emailed to the prospective student within (2) weeks from submission of all materials.
     
  • What documentation is required for admission into the program?

    Answer Seven:     A letter of good standing from the student's home law school is required for processing the application. The letter should specifically state that the applicant has, or will have, completed one full year of law studies by the time the program takes place. The letter should also specifically state that the student is IN GOOD STANDING at his/her home law school. While no specific grade point average is required to attend any of Widener's institutes, as a general rule a G.P.A. of 2.3 or above is acceptable.

    Note: Students on academic probation, or, students who believe they may be placed on academic probation may attend the program on a case-by-case basis, and do so AT THEIR OWN RISK.

    A fully completed application (with all information for each question answered), as well as a letter of good standing from the student's home law school must be forwarded to the International Programs Coordinator. ÊApplicants are not requried to pay a fee upon submission of application, but accepted applicants must forward a non-refundable deposit by April 15, 2004.
     
  • What, if any, are the enrollment limitations for each program?

    Answer Eight:     Currently, there are no specific limitations on the number of students who enroll. On average, previous years' attendance has been around (20) for each program. In order to ensure the highest quality instruction, a general rule of thumb is to set a limit of (30) for each program.
     
  • How many courses can I take? Will credit for the course(s) be applicable at my home institution? Will participation in a Widener Summer Program accelerate my graduation?

    Answer Nine:     All Widener summer programs allow for a maximum of (6) credits, or (2) courses to be taken during a the program.

    Widener courses may be credited toward your law degree at an ABA-approved institution if your law school accepts credits for summer programs. A transcript of grades will be sent to each student and to their home school. Acceptance of any credit or grade for any course is subject to determination by the student's home school.

    It is unlikely that participation in a foreign summer program may be used to accelerate graduation. Consult with your law school's registrar.

    Information about housing in Venice is available on the housing link of the Venice website, and more information can be obtained by contacting Professor Susan Raeker-Jordan at: Susan.Raeker-Jordan@law.widener.edu.
     
  • Will there be an orientation both before, and at the beginning of each program? What plans are in place if I can not attend the orientation?

    Answer Eleven:     Widener University School of Law will conduct an orientation day on its Delaware campus for students who will attend the Venice program. Students not able to attend scheduled orientations will be sent a VHS videotape of the orientation for their information.

    Each program will conduct an orientation day on the first day of their respective summer programs. Specific scheduling information will be forthcoming in respective program's student manuals (with plans to have student manuals available on the web).
     
  • When is the application/fee due to Widener Law School?

    Answer Twelve:     The application deadline for all 2004 Summer Abroad Law Institutes is April 15, 2004. If students know they want to attend a Widener program, but anticipate that their application may arrive after the April 15, 2004, deadline, contact the International Programs Coordinator at (302) 477-2248 to request an exception to policy. A $50 non-refundable deposit is due before April 15, 2004 and the balance of tuition is due by June 8, 2004.

    For Widener students utilizing financial aid: you need to see a financial aid counselor, and ensure the proper forms are filled out and processed, so that the Bursar's Office knows the money will be disbursed prior to the start of classes (the disbursement is normally 10 days before the start of classes).

    For non-Widener students utilizing financial aid: the home school processes a consortium agreement with Widener Law and paperwork is exchanged. The Widener University School of Law Bursar's must receive the portion of your loan disbursement dedicated to paying tuition at least (10) business days prior to the start of the program on June 27, 2004, which is June 8, 2004.

    Exceptions to policy are certainly granted in cases where the financial aid disbursement is delayed through no fault of the student.

  • What procedures are in place if I need to withdraw from the program?

    Answer Fifteen:     Should any student need to withdraw from the Venice program due to an emergency, Widener Law School will work with the student on a reimbursement schedule of academic related funds. Withdraw from the program(s) will be handled on a case-by-case basis beginning with the Director of the Program, and in coordination with the Dean of Students, Widener University School of Law.

    In the unlikely event of a cancellation, applicants will be notified immediately by electronic mail and telephone, and offered the choice for a full refund or acceptance in our other summer international programs
     
  • Are the facilities associated with each program accessible to persons with special physical needs?

    Answer Sixteen:     The facilities at the Law School of the University of Technology, Sydney (UTS), are relatively accessible to students with special physical needs. Interested students should be aware that facilities abroad are not as well developed with regard to ramps, wider doors/elevators, and the like. While UTS strives to remain accessible to all students, those students requiring more specific assistance should contact the program's co-director, Professor Christine McDermott at: Christine.M.McDermott@law.widener.edu.
