Sydney, Australia Summer Abroad Law Institute: Faculty

Patrick Keyzer

Widener University School of Law is very privileged to have one of Australia's most renowned legal scholars joining our faculty in 2004. Patrick Keyzer is a Senior Lecturer at UTS. He teaches and writes on Constitutional Law, Intellectual Property, and Comparative Law. He has been the Executive Associate to Sir Gerard Brennan, Chief Justice of Australia and he has also participated in litigation on behalf of the North Australian Aboriginal Legal Aid Service. He has written books about intellectual property in Thailand and in Indonesia and he rewrote the Constitutional Law title of Halsbury's Laws of Australia, the nation's premiere legal encyclopedia. Last year, Mr. Keyzer received the UTS Vice-Chancellor's Social Justice and Human Rights Award. Professor Keyzer has been involved with Widener's program for years and has been very instrumental in its success.

Christine M. McDermott

Widener University Faculty. Christine M. McDermott earned her B.A. in History at the University of Delaware and her J.D. from Dickinson School of Law. After serving as the Chief Prosecutor for the City of Wilmington, Delaware, she was the Executive Director of the state crime agency (LEAA) under two Delaware Governors, working to institute victim services, improvements in law enforcement and court administration, as well as reforms in adult and juvenile sentencing. She later Directed the Delaware Council on Crime and Justice, a private non-profit agency, before coming to the Delaware Civil Clinic at Widener. Under her supervision, Widener students represent victims of domestic violence in protection from abuse, divorce and custody cases. Professor McDermott was the 1999 recipient of the Delaware State Bar Women's Leadership Award. She teaches in the areas of Criminal Law, Domestic Violence and Professional Responsibility.

Susan Raeker-Jordan

Widener University Faculty. Susan Raeker-Jordan has a B.A. from the University of Maine and a J.D. from the University of South Carolina. She teaches courses on Products Liability and Torts and her principal research interests are in products liability, preemption of state products liability suits, and capital punishment. She has practiced in the tort area, written articles on products liability preemption issues, and recently contributed an entry on a United States Supreme Court preemption case for the Oxford Companion to the Supreme Court.
