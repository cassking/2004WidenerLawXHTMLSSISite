Harrisburg Civil Clinic

Harrisburg, Pennsylvania

A good many Widener students come from Pennsylvania and return there to practice law after graduation. The Pennsylvania Civil Clinic has been introducing Widener students to the Pennsylvania Bar and Courts since 1985. Over the years, the Clinic has refined its practice, and now specializes in two areas of law.

In handling Bankruptcy cases before the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania (Philadelphia), Widener student attorneys help provide relief from sometimes crushing, disheartening debt for indigent clients in Delaware County, Pennsylvania. Student attorneys conduct interviews and do research, develop strategies with Professor Nathaniel Nichols, negotiate with creditors and proceed in federal court with Chapter 7, Chapter 13 and related cases.

Often those Bankruptcy cases come about because of circumstances in the families of Clinic clients. Loss of a job can result in foreclosure on a home, leading to a downward spiral of divorce and sometimes child custody disputes. Widener student attorneys thus pursue Family Law cases in Pennsylvania state courts on behalf of Clinic clients. In addition to the regular caseload, students can opt for ample regular court experience by electing to spend one morning each week in Support Court with the Delaware County Office of Support Enforcement.
