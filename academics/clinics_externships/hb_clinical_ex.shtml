Harrisburg Clinical Externship Program

Harrisburg, Pennsylvania

Externships offer students who have completed two years of school the opportunity to earn credit for experiential learning. Students are placed in settings outside the Law School where the primary responsibility of supervision lies with an attorney in the host agency. In the Clinical Externship Program, students assist attorneys for the government, courts or not-for-profit organizations as they carry out their particular duties.

A weekly classroom component where skills issues are presented expose students to the practice of law and allow them to reflect upon the role of the lawyer in our society. Students may obtain two, three or four credit hours per semester and are required to do between 10 and 20 hours per week of work in their placement. As the state capitol of Pennsylvania, students are presented with an endless array of options which include such placements as:

  • Dauphin County District Attorney and Public Defender
  • Supreme Court of Pennsylvania Administration Offices
  • PA Department of State - Office of Chief Counsel
  • PA Office of Inspector General
  • PA Office of Attorney General
  • Pennsylvania Human Relations Commission
  • Pennsylvania House of Representatives
  • USDA
  • York Immigration Court
  • Clean Air Counsel
  • Lewisburg Prison Project
  • City of Harrisburg - Solicitor
  • Placements in the Clinical Externship Program qualify for students seeking to fulfill requirements of the Law and Government Institute.
