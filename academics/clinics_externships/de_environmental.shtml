Environmental Law & Natural Resources Clinic

Wilmington, Delaware

Widener students from the Environmental Law and Natural Resources Clinic fan out throughout the Delaware Valley to keep an eye on polluters and governmental agencies to make sure that environmental laws are enforced. Student attorneys are taking air and water samples, consulting with scientists, engineers and administrative agencies, and researching documents for violations of environmental laws such as the Federal Clean Water Act, the Clean Air Act, the Endangered Species Act, the Hazardous Waste Law, the National Environmental Policy Act, and other federal and state laws.

Widener students work with a regional public interest law firm in litigating these cases. They interview clients and witnesses, investigate polluters, and research the applicable laws. They discuss and prepare case strategies with Widener Professor Jim May and other attorneys, and then draft pleadings, motions and agreements, and appear in courts throughout the Mid-Atlantic region on behalf of Clinic clients.
