Student Records

The Registrar's Office is deeply involved with the main reason that students come to Widener University School of Law - to take courses and to obtain degrees. We are responsible for maintaining the academic records of all current and former students at the University. We track students' progress towards the completion of their degrees, and are responsible for making sure that students have completed all of the requirements necessary to complete their degrees.

If you need a transcript or certification of enrollment, need to change your name or address, or report a change in your employment status, please complete the appropriate forms found on the Registrar Downloads & Forms page.

Transcript Requests

All requests for transcripts must be made in writing to the Office of the Registrar. Requests must include the student's full name, Social Security number, graduation date (if applicable), number of copies needed, the address where transcripts should be sent, and the student's original signature. There is a $2.00 charge per copy payable by check, cash or money order. Please send requests to the appropriate campus.

Loan Deferments

The Office of the Registrar signs and seals the deferment forms that students receive from their individual lenders. Matriculated students must turn in these forms for completion to the Registrar's Office. The Office will mail the forms directly to the lending institutions.

Graduation Analysis

The Office of the Registrar is responsible for evaluating which students are candidates for graduation. By the fall of the students' final year, candidates will have completed a graduation packet. One of the items included in the packet is a graduation self-analysis form. Working with the Registrar staff, each student can determine their status toward graduation. Submitting these forms to the Office of the Registrar in a timely manner ensures the graduation process runs smoothly.

Bar Certification

At the beginning of the last semester of law school, candidates for graduation will turn their attention to applying to sit for one or more bar examinations. Most states require you to write a letter of intent. In the letter to the state bar you will provide vital information about yourself; including, your full name, social security number and anticipated graduation date. If you have any questions or need addresses to various bars, please come to the Office of the Registrar, Bar Programs Office or call the state bar directly. Additionally, information can be found on the websites for the specific bars.
