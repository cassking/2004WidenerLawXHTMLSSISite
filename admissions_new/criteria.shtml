Admissions Criteria & Requirements

All School of Law admission applications are reviewed by the Admissions Committee. The applicant's undergraduate grade point average and the Law School Admission Test (LSAT - http://www.lsac.org) score are two important considerations in determining the likelihood of success in legal training. The Committee also considers other factors that may indicate an ability to successfully complete the requirements for graduation. These include graduate study, work experience, extracurricular activities, community involvement and life experiences. These factors may be articulated to the Admissions committee through the submission of a resume, letters of recommendation and a well-written personal statement.

The School of Law is committed to creating a diverse student population. To this end, it encourages members of minority groups traditionally underrepresented in the legal profession to apply for and accept admission. Appropriate consideration is given for educational or economic disadvantage.

IMPORTANT: Please review all application for admission requirements including: LSAT requirements, Letters of recommendation requirements, and transcript requirements.
