How to Apply for Admissions: Application Checklist

  • Obtain a registration packet from Law Services, Box 2000, Newtown, PA 18940-0998 or at the Law School Admission Council site: http://www.lsac.org.
     
  • Take the The Law School Admission Test (LSAT) and register for the The Law School Data Assembly Service (LSDAS).

    Do you need more information about our LSAT requirements?
     
  • Complete, sign and mail to the Admissions Office the attached application form with the nonrefundable $60 application fee. If you are applying to both campuses, only one application fee is required. However, applicants applying to both campuses should be prepared to accept admission to the campus designated by the Law School. Applicants applying to both campuses have waived their choice of campus. Please include Social Security number or Law Services I.D. number on all checks, made payable to Widener University. Application fee waived if you apply online before December 31, 2004. Apply Online now!
     
  • Have all undergraduate, graduate and professional schools attended transmit official transcripts of applicant's records to LSDAS.

    Do you need more information about our transcript requirements?
     
  • Submit all letters of recommendation to LSDAS.

    Do you need more information about our letters of recommendation requirements?
     
  • J.D./Psy.D. applicants must submit additional application materials. The deadline for J.D./Psy.D. application is February 1. To receive a complete application packet, contact:

    Law-Psychology Program
    Institute for Graduate Clinical Psychology
    Widener University
    One University Place
    Chester, PA 19013-5792
    (610) 499-1209
     
  • Foreign applicants seeking admission to the Juris Doctor program may be required to submit an official report of their score on the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL). Foreign applicants may be required to submit a translation of their foreign transcript if the grading system is not comparable to the American grading system. Additional information regarding the requirements for foreign applicants may be obtained by contacting the Admissions Office.
