Frequently Asked Questions About Admissions to Widener University School of Law

  • Can I apply on-line?
  • Are letters of recommendation required?
  • May I submit additional information with my application?
  • When will I receive a decision?
  • What are the median LSAT and undergraduate grade point average for the Fall 2003 entering class?
  • How does Widener consider multiple LSAT scores?

  • What should I include in response to the character & fitness questions on my application?
  • Will I have to pay a seat deposit if admitted?

The Answers:

  • When is the application deadline?
    Answer One:     The application deadline for Fall admission (Delaware and Harrisburg Campuses) is May 15th. Offers of admission are extended on a rolling basis so it is beneficial to apply as early as possible.
     
  • What is the application fee?
    Answer Two:     The application fee is $60.00. (The application fee is WAIVED for all applications received on or before December 31, 2004.) APPLY ONLINE now.
     
  • Can I apply to both of your campuses on one application?
    Answer Three:     Yes, simply check the Harrisburg and Delaware boxes on the top of the application and send in only one $60.00 application fee. Applicants applying to both campuses should be prepared to accept admission at the campus designated by the Law School. While applying to both campuses does not increase an applicant's chances of acceptance, applicants applying to both campuses waive their choice of campus.
     
  • Can I apply on-line?
    Answer Four:     Yes. Online applications are retrieved each day by the Admissions Office staff. APPLY ONLINE now.
     
  • Are letters of recommendation required?
    Answer Five:     No, however, submission of letters of recommendation is strongly suggested. You may send up to three letters of recommendation to Widener with your application or under separate cover or to Law Services letter of recommendation service.
     
  • May I submit additional information with my application?
    Answer Six:     Yes. A personal statement is highly encouraged. A resume is also acceptable.
     
  • When will I receive a decision?
    Answer Seven:     We begin making admissions decisions in December. After December, a decision is typically made within 4-6 weeks after submission of your application. Decisions for the summer Trial Admissions Program are made in March and April.
     
  • What are the median LSAT and undergraduate grade point average for the Fall 2003 entering class?
    Answer Eight:     The median LSAT score is 151 and the median GPA is 3.10.
     
  • How does Widener consider multiple LSAT scores?
    Answer Nine:     Widener will average multiple test scores. However, if a reasonable explanation is provided for discrepant scores, more weight will be given to the higher score. This explanation should not be included in a personal statement but instead be provided with the application.
     
  • Is one undergraduate major of study more competitive for admission than another?
    Answer Ten:     I The Admissions Committee is looking for a well-rounded individual who has excelled at academic study regardless of the major. No one major is more suited for law school study than another.
     
  • What should I include in response to the character & fitness questions on my application?
    Answer Eleven:     You should include all arrests, charges and convictions for all felony and misdemeanor offenses other than a minor traffic violation, including juvenile offenses and records that may be expunged, as well as probation and parole terms. You should also include any actions taken against you by licensing boards. When applying to the take the bar exam, the State Board of Bar Examiners will review original applications for admission to law school. If a discrepancy is found, even if it is unintentional, this may preclude you from sitting for the bar exam. If you are unsure about including a particular incident or record, you should contact your State Board of Bar Examiners for further clarification; however, it is always in your best interest to err on the side of full disclosure.
     
  • Will I have to pay a seat deposit if admitted?
    Answer Twelve:     Yes, a $400.00 non-refundable seat deposit is due on May 1st. Students accepted after April 15th should refer to their acceptance letter for specific seat deposit instructions. All seat deposits are credited toward tuition upon matriculation.
     
  • Is Widener School of Law accredited?
    Answer Thirteen:     Widener University School of Law is fully accredited by the American Bar Association (ABA) and The Association of American Law Schools (AALS).
