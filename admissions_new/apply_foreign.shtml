How to Apply If You Are a Foreign/International Student

Seeking Admission to the J.D. Program:

The School of Law is committed to creating as diverse a student population as possible. Foreign/International applicants seeking admission to the Juris Doctor program may be required to submit an official report of their score on the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL). Foreign applicants are required to submit a translation and analysis of their foreign transcripts. Foreign educated applicants should contact World Education Services at (212) 966-6311 or http://www.wes.org or a similar agency to obtain information and instructions regarding transcript analysis and translation. Additional information regarding the requirements for foreign applicants may be obtained by contacting the Admissions Office.

Seeking Admission to the LL.M. Program (Foreign Law School Graduates):

Foreign law graduates seeking admissions into LL.M. programs are also required to submit an official report of their score on the Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) . Widener's institution code is 2642. Additional information regarding requirements for international applicants may be obtained by contacting the Graduate Programs Office.
