Outside Scholarships

Financial assistance is also offered directly to students by various private and public organizations who have no involvement with the Law School. Such donors include county and state bar associations, law spouses' groups, veterans' organizations, church and ethnic organizations, college or university alumni associations and selected corporations with tuition programs for employees. Applicants should also consult various financial aid directories available at most public and college libraries. Awards from these sources must be reported to the Financial Aid Office as soon as they are secured.

Donor Scholarships:
Donor scholarships are endowments established through the generosity of alumni, friends, faculty, foundations, law firms and corporations. Since the founding of Widener University School of Law, our law students have benefited from the significant gifts of our many donors. The Law School is deeply grateful for their loyal support. Private Loan Programs

Various lenders offer private education loans to cover expenses not covered by federal and institutional assistance. Students may generally borrow up to the estimated cost of attendance for each academic year (minus other aid received) in these programs. Special Alternative Loan Program

CitiAssist loans are private credit based education loans designed to help you fill the gap between the financial aid you have and the money you need for college.

The Widener special program provided you with these unique benefits:

  • No fees ever! -- You get 100% of the money you borrow and there are no origination fees, guarantee fees or repayment fees.
  • Simple Pricing -- Prime +0%* - You get the same low rate over the life of your loan, with no surprising interest rate increases when you graduate or enter repayment. CitiAssist Fills the Gap between the financial aid you already have and the real cost of school. You can borrow up to the cost of education less other financial aid you receive.
  • Flexible Repayment Options No Payments Required While in School
  • Cosigner Release -- If you do require a cosigner, your cosigner may be released from the loan after you have made 24 consecutive monthly payments on time.
  • APR Example: If you borrowed $6,000 and had an in-school period plus a post graduation grace period totaling 38 months followed by a repayment period of 144 months, and if the Prime Rate maintained a constant 7.75%, the APR would be 8.36%. Also, the principal amount upon entering repayment would be $7,662.14, the monthly payment would be $86.11, and the finance charge over the life of the loan would be $6,400.41. Payments are assumed deferred during the in-school and grace period for a total of 38 months. Repayment begins at the end of the grace period and is assumed to be for 144 months. The interest rate may vary and is indexed to the Prime Rate as published in The Wall Street Journal. As of January 11, 1999, the published Prime Rate was 7.75%. The APR will increase if the Prime Rate increases and would result in higher monthly payments, an increase in the number of scheduled payments, or both.
