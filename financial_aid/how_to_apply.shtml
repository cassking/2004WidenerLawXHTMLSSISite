How To Apply For Financial Aid If You Are A New Student

Following acceptance by the Admissions Committee, each applicant automatically receives the detailed instructions and forms required to apply for financial assistance. All financial aid applicants must file a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) and the Institutional Data Form. The Free Application can be done on paper or via the Internet at http://www.fafsa.ed.gov. Be sure to indicate the school code for the campus you are to attend. The code for the Delaware campus is B04724. The code for the Harrisburg campus is E00656.

Applicants intending to borrow through the Federal Stafford Loan Program must also complete a Master Promissory Note. More information can be found on the Federal Types of Financial Aid Page, under the Special Widener Loan Program heading. Financial aid for entering students is awarded on a rolling basis. Each applicant is evaluated for aid only after all required forms have been received and the applicant has been formally admitted to the Law School. Financial aid awards are allocated on a rolling basis, it is recommended that applicants for admission begin the process of applying for aid as soon after application for admission as possible. Applicants whose information is received after funds have been committed are placed on a waiting list to replace aid recipients who withdraw or forfeit their awards.

How To Apply For Financial Aid If You Are A Returning/Current Student

 

Returning students will be provided with the financial aid packet via their on-campus mailboxes.The priority filing deadline for returning students is April 15. Students whose FAFSA results and Data Form are received by the Financial Aid Office by April 15 will receive priority in the awarding process and in determining eligibility for Federal Perkins Loan and Federal Work-Study funds and Widener's Institutional Need Based Dean's Grant Program.

The Financial Aid Office strongly encourages the use to the Internet to complete your Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA). The FAFSA can be completed at http://www.fafsa.ed.gov. You can access your renewal application by using the PIN number that was mailed to you in December by the Department of Education. If you have misplaced your PIN number, you may request a duplicate from the same site. Duplicate PIN numbers will be mailed within 10 days or provided to you via email in 48 hours. The Widener University School of Law Institutional Data Form can be downloaded on this page (please see above).

Students planning to enroll for a summer session and requiring financial aid to be processed must complete a Summer Intent Form. Forms are provided each year in your financial aid packet. Additional forms are available in the Financial Aid Office. Students requesting financial aid for the summer session(s) must schedule an appointment with a financial aid counselor. Evening and phone appointments are available on a limited basis.

It is vitally important that students submit all required forms to the Financial Aid Office in a timely manner. Students are reminded to read carefully and complete all financial aid forms correctly. Failure to do so will cause delays in processing and may result in the loss of aid.

How To Apply For Financial Aid If You Are A Transfer Student

Transfer students are encouraged to submit all required financial aid information and forms simultaneously with their application for admission so that, aid applications can be reviewed directly after admission. Please see instructions for New Students.

How To Apply For Financial Aid If You Are A Visiting Student

Visiting students are not eligible for federal financial aid from the Law School. Visiting students should contact the Financial Aid Office at their home institution to arrange for financial assistance.

Financial Aid Process for Widener Students visiting another institution Widener Students visiting another institution may elect to have their financial aid processed by Widener's Financial Aid Office by having the school they will be attending complete a Widener University School of Law Consortium Agreement. Consortium Agreements are available in the Financial Aid Office. Financial aid will only be processed for students who have been approved by the Office of Student Academic Affairs to visit. Widener's Financial Aid Office will process both federal and private financial aid for students visiting during the regular academic year (fall and spring semesters). Students choosing to visit during the summer will have private loans processed as an institutional policy.

Financial Aid Verification Process

The Department of Education annually selects a number of financial aid applications for a process of verification. This process was developed to ensure that students are receiving appropriate award amounts and that income and asset information is being reported correctly. Students selected for this process will be required to submit a copy of their most recent tax return (signed) with all attached schedules and W-2's and a verification worksheet which can be obtained from the Financial Aid Office. After an initial review, students will be notified of any additional documentation required to complete their financial aid files.
