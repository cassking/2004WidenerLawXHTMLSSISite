Suggested Readings for the Beginning Law Student

Listed below are selected books that you may find valuable in preparing for the law school experience. You can find these in our library and at most book stores.

We invite you to browse our web site for more information about the Legal Information Center. Be sure to look at Information for New Students, which will have lots of useful web sites that will be helpful in preparing you for law school and providing information on what to expect. We also have an expanded list of suggested readings, if you have some extra reading time this summer.

On behalf of the Legal Information Center, best of luck on a successful legal career.

Click on the call number to find the book in the Legal Information Center.

  • Jonathan Harr. A Civil Action New York: Randon House, 1995. KF228.A7 H37 1995 Description: This book won the National Book Critics Award for nonfiction. It describes a lawsuit brought by the families of childhood leukemia victims against two corporate giants accused of industrial pollution.
  • Bob Woodward & Scott Armstrong The Brethren: Inside the Supreme Court New York: Simon & Schuster, 1979 KF8742 .W66 1979 Description: Read this for an interesting in-depth inner look at the U.S. Supreme Court. Don't confuse it with the popular fictional book by the same name. May be hard to get at a book store but most libraries have it.
  • Anthony Lewis Gideon's Trumpet New York: Vintage Books, 1964 (& later reprints) KF9646 .L48 Description: An indigent drifter who was convicted of a crime without legal representation wrote a note to the U.S. Supreme Court, who took his case. This case was the reason indigent defendants facing serious criminal charges have a right to legal counsel at the state's expense.
  • Helene S. Shapo Law School Without Fear: Strategies for Success Westbury, N.Y.: Foundation Press, 1996 KF240 .S52 1996 Description: When the son of two law professors decided to go to law school, they wrote a book to help him through the trials and tribulations.
  • Scott Turow One L New York: Putnam, 1977 KF373.T88 A33 Description: Before Scott Turow became famous for writing legal fiction, he wrote this nonfiction description of his first year at Harvard Law School.
