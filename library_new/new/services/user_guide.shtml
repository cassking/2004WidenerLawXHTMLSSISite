Library User Guide

Overview of Services and Research Resources

Welcome to the Widener University Legal Information Center. The following will help you find information on this site.

Who are we?

The Legal Information Center is the major law library in the state of Delaware and is recognized as one of the major law libraries in the region. The LIC's site is designed to be a convenient, online research resource for our students, and alumni. It is also a convenient way to: inquire about workshops in various subjects through our Library Training program; request Interlibrary Loans; conduct Legal and General Research and get Reference questions answered. Additionally, both the Wilmington and Harrisburg sites are designated as United States Government Depository Libraries. Depository status enables selection and free of charge receipt of a broad spectrum of government documents and reports, which supplement the federal administrative materials. Would you like to learn more about our collection? Please visit our Site Map, which provides you with links to every page in the LIC website.

Where are we?

In addition to this web site, the Widener Law Library supports and maintains thousands of volumes of legal materials, a full-time Information and research Staff at its campuses in both Wilmington, Delaware and Harrisburg, Pennsylvania.

How to use our site

Our site offers links to State, Local and Federal Legal Resources, a wide variety of Search Engines, and more. All sites listed are evaluated by Widener University Law librarians, and annotations make it easy to decide which sites are right for your needs. In addition, for students and alumni, we currently provide online access to a number of Subscription (paid) Legal Periodical Databases.

Recommended Tools for Legal Research Online

  • Widener Law University Catalog (LibCat). LibCat, Widener University's online catalog, is accessible through the Internet and includes the library collections of the Legal Information Centers on both the Wilmington and Harrisburg campuses, as well as the Wolfgram Memorial Library on the Chester campus.
  • Explore the Library Catalog
  • Legal and Nonlegal Research Links Pages: We have compiled a comprehensive list of links to State, Local and Federal Legal Resources, a wide variety of Search Engines, and more.
  • General Information about Services: Our staff is available during library hours to answer reference questions. Check out our Ask a Librarian page for additional circulation, access or reference information.
