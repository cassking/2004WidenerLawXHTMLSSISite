Exams on Reserve

Working through a prior exam may be a good way to prepare yourself for your current exam. Prior exams are available on reserve at the Circulation Desk. Not every professor has exams on file and not every subject is covered. Course reserve items may be checked out for three hours. Course Reserve items (excluding exams) checked out after 10:00 p.m. will be due back by 10:00 a.m. Some exams are also available online. View the exams. Please note that there are some exams available on reserve that are not online so be sure to check in the library, especially for older exams.

Computer-Based Lessons and Tutorials

CALI (Center for Computer-Assisted Legal Instruction) is a non-profit consortium of 175 U.S. Law Schools. As a member of the CALI consortium, Widener is able to offer students access to the CALI Library of Exercises. These exercises are a collection of over 100 interactive lessons written by law faculty and law librarians in a variety of legal subject areas. They can be beneficial in helping you test your understanding of an area of law.

CALI is available over the Internet at www.cali.org. Access the lessons by clicking on "For Students" on the CALI homepage and then on "CALI Lessons." Select a subject area and then a lesson and proceed according to the instructions provided. If you have Internet access at home, the exercises can be downloaded or run directly from the CALI web site. Follow the instructions on the screen.

Need to know the password? Have any questions? Call the reference desk at 477-2114 (Delaware campus); 541-3934 (Harrisburg campus) or use the Ask a Librarian email reference.

Access CALI from the lab computers by clicking on the "Start" button; select "Programs" and then "CALI."

Supplementary Study Materials

In addition to hornbooks and nutshells located in the Reserve room, the Legal Information Center also has other supplementary materials which may be helpful to you as you prepare for your exam.

Matthew Bender publishes a "Legal Text Series" which aims to provide clearly written and authoritative coverage of every major concept in a subject area. The titles of these books begin with "Understanding." So for legal topics you will have Understanding Civil Procedure, Understanding Contracts, etc. Click here for a list of available copies.

Another option is the "Examples and Explanations" series. Available for a number of subject areas including criminal law, administrative law and constitutional law; these books not only summarize the law, but also analyze and explain rules and doctrines. To see a list of available copies visit this page of the Library Catalog.
