Legal Education Institute (LEI) Programs: Admissions

Admission to each program offered by the Legal Education Institute is competitive. Factors involved in the admission process include academic record, Scholastic Aptitude Test (SAT) scores, extracurricular activities, letters of recommendation, and work experience. Applicants who have significant work experience should consult with the Legal Education Institute office to determine if the SAT is required. Prospective students are encouraged to arrange for a personal interview and should submit the application and academic transcript as early as possible.

Application Instructions

  • STEP ONE: Submit completed, signed application with the non-refundable $30 application fee made payable to Widener University Law Center, or, apply ONLINE now! Please include social security number on all checks.
  • STEP TWO: Request high school, college, or university to forward an official transcript of academic record. Those who have attended college need only send official college transcript.
  • STEP THREE: Forward results of the Scholastic Aptitude Test (SAT) or submit a letter requesting that the SAT requirement be waived. Our SAT code is 2642.
     
  • STEP FOUR: Send three letters of recommendation directly to the Legal Education Institute.
     
  • STEP FIVE: All Legal Nurse Consultant Applicants also must submit a copy of their current RN license.
     
  • STEP SIX: A personal interview with an advisor is required for all transfer students.
