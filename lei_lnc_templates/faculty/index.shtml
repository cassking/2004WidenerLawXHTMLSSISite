Legal Education Institute Faculty

The Legal Education Institute at Widener University has earned a reputation for excellent academic programs. Our distinguished faculty and experienced staff are devoted to providing each student with the skills and opportunities necessary to develop individual talents. Below, meet our faculty.

Full-Time Faculty

Susan S. Bacastow, Esq. + | -

  • Ms. Bacastow graduated from Millersville University and was awarded her B.S. in 1976. She went on to graduate cum laude, Spring Honors Admission Program, from Widener University School of Law with her J.D. in 1990. Ms. Bacastow received a Certificate in Estate Planning from Villanova University School of Law in 2003 and will receive her LL.M. in Taxation in 2004.

    Ms. Bacastow's bar admissions include the Pennsylvania and New Jersey Supreme Courts, and the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania.

    Currently Ms. Bacastow is self-employed, practicing primarily in the area of estate planning, and is a Professor of Law at the Legal Education Institute of Widener University, teaching Business Organizations, Civil Practice, Contracts, Ethics, Introduction to Law, Torts, and Wills and Trusts.

    Ms. Bacastow served as a Certified Legal Intern for Delaware Volunteer Legal Services, Inc., as a judicial law clerk to the Honorable Michael Joseph Melody, Jr. in the Chester County Court of Common Pleas, and to the Honorable Richard A. Powers, III in the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania, and as a civil litigation attorney for the Offices of Dolchin, Slotkin & Todd.

Amy L. Feeney, Esq. + | -

  • Ms. Feeney graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Human Resource Management from Wilmington College in 1992, and her Associate of Science in Paralegal Studies from Austin Community College in 1995.

    She worked as a paralegal for Young, Clement, Rivers & Tisdale; Garwood, McKenna & McKenna; and Advanced Micro Devices, during which time she earned the Certified Legal Assistant (CLA) designation awarded by the National Association of Legal Assistants (NALA).

    Ms. Feeney graduated cum laude from Widener University School of Law with her J.D. in 2002. Currently, she is full-time faculty and Special Projects Coordinator with the Legal Education Institute of Widener University.

Eileen A. Grena, Esq. + | -

  • Ms. Grena graduated from American University with a B.S. in May 1988, from Widener University School of Law with a J.D. in May 1992, and from the University of Delaware School of Nursing with an R.N., B.S.N., in February 1997.

    Ms. Grena is currently Assistant Director at Widener University Legal Education Institute, and adjunct professor for Widener University School of Law as well as a practicing nurse consultant.

    Ms. Grena has taught paralegal studies at Manor Junior College, and has Neonatal Intensive Care Nurse at Christiana Care Health System. She has also served as the Mental Health/Mental Retardation County Solicitor at the Chester County Mental Health Court, Assistant Counsel for Jeanes Hospital of Philadelphia, and as an Arbitration Judge for Chester County.

Cecilia M. McCormick, Esq. + | -

  • Cecilia M. McCormick is Assistant Dean and Director of the Legal Education Institute at Widener University Law Center, Delaware Campus.

    Assistant Dean McCormick received her Bachelor's Degree in Management/Marketing from St. Joseph's University in 1981, her Paralegal Certificate from Widener University in 1983, and her J.D. from Widener University School of Law in 1991.

    Following graduation from law school, Assistant Dean McCormick joined the law firm of Peter J. Rohana, Jr., where she served as a paralegal, law clerk and attorney over a ten-year period. From 1992-1996, Assistant Dean McCormick worked for the firm of Sereni and Lunardi, Broomall, on a full-time basis, and on a part-time basis from 1996-2001.

    Assistant Dean McCormick served as Assistant Professor and Assistant Dean for the School of Business Administration at Widener University from 1996-1998. Thereafter, she resumed the practice of law doing special projects with PECO Energy and The Vanguard Group.

    Assistant Dean McCormick is admitted to practice in the State of Pennsylvania. She teaches in the areas of Real Estate, Wills and Trusts, Business Law, and Family Law. Assistant Dean McCormick is a member of the American Bar Association, the Pennsylvania Bar Association, and the Delaware County Bar Association.
