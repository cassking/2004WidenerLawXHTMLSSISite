About This Website

The Widener University School of Law website aims to be accessible to all users including people with disabilities for whom the internet can be a valuable tool. Technical tools exist which convert text on web pages to the spoken word and thereby make information accessible to people with disabilities, particularly those with visual impairment and reading difficulties.

The degree to which web pages are accessible to users with disabilities is defined in terms of priority levels and all new pages within the Widener University School of Law website will be required to be universally accessible at the priority 1 level. It is our goal that all pages within this site be be developed with a view to achieving accessibility at priority levels 2 and 3.

For more information please visit:
WAI - http://www.w3.org/WAI/
The WAI (Web Accessibility Initiative), in coordination with organizations around the world, pursues accessibility of the Web through five primary areas of work: technology, guidelines, tools, education and outreach, and research and development.

About Accessing the Widener University School of Law Website

Below is a list of five informational items related to accessing the Widener University School of Law website:

1. Description of the overall structure of the Widener University School of Law website:

The Widener University School of Law web pages, except the home page, are divided into the following five sections:

  1. The top navigation bar. It consists of links to the main sections of the Widener University School of Law website.
  2. The left navigation menu.This is where the screen reader will begin to read first. It contains links to the subtopics within the area being viewed.
  3. The breadcrumb navigation directly under the top navigation bar. It contains a trail of links to detail where you are in relationto the site's homepage.
  4. The content. This is where the content for a page is displayed.
  5. The footer. Through the use of the icons at the bottom of each page, every page page can be tested for valid xhtml code and accessibilty.

2. Description of tools to navigate the Widener University School of Law website:

  1. "Alt" tags. Picture links and images have alt tags, which are text describing the links and images that a screen reader can read.
  2. Link "titles". Links have title tags, which are text describing the links that a screen reader can read.
  3. Access keys. They exist for all main sections of the website and they allow access to different parts of the site using only keyboard strokes.People with limited mobility may have a hard time controlling a mouse to click on links.Access keys enable users to select the appropriate key on their keyboards and navigate to a particular link.

3. Stylesheets:

  1. Stylesheets are used to format this website.
  2. In the near future, we are hoping that using stylesheets will allow us to provide alternative stylesheets in order to offer a choice of font sizes when needed.

4. Adobe Acrobat: If you use a screen reader, you can get more information about accessing Acrobat (PDF)files from the Adobe web site: http://www.adobe.com/products/acrobat/access_onlinetools.html.

5. We are working to make the Widener University School of Law website as accessible as possible. If you have helpful suggestions or comments, please email them to the webmaster at cir0300@mail.widener.edu. Thank you.
