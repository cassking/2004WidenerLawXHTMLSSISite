How to Change Text (Font) Size

In the interests of allowing users with very large or small computer screens, poor eyesight, or wireless handhelds (small screens) to more comfortably view our webpages, the Research Library's webpages use your browser's default font size rather than a fixed font size (i.e. 12pt). This page lists instructions on how to change the default font size for the most common browsers.

Internet Explorer 5+

In the menu at the top of your browser window labelled 'View' there is an item labeled 'Text Size', which opens a sub-menu. This sub-menu should have the items 'Largest', 'Larger', 'Medium', 'Smaller', and 'Smallest'. One of these items should be marked; this is your current font size. Selecting a higher font size will make the letters on the website larger (and possibly easier to read), while a lower font size will make the letters smaller, letting you see more of the web page at once.

Netscape Navigator 4.x

In the menu at the top of your browser window labelled 'View' there are two items labeled 'Increase Font' and 'Decrease Font'. Selecting 'Increase Font' will make the letters on the web-page larger (and possibly easier to read). Selecting 'Decrease Font' will make the letters smaller, allowing you to view more of the website in one window. Alternately, you can hold down the keyboard key labelled 'Ctrl' and then press ']' to increase the font size or '[' to decrease the font size.

Netscape 6+ or Mozilla

In the menu at the top of your browser window labelled 'View' there is an item labeled 'Text Zoom' with a percentage in parentheses. When you select this item a sub-menu will open which gives you a number of self-explanatory options for changing your font size. Alternately, you can hold down the keyboard key labelled 'Ctrl' and press the keys '+' or '-' to increase or decrease your font size, respectively. Increasing your font size will make the text on the webpage larger (and possibly easier to read), while decreasing your font size will make the text smaller, enabling you to see more of the webpage without scrolling.

Opera

On the browser menu, select View, then Zoom to choose an appropriate factor. Please note that image sizes may be modified using this option.

AOL

Select My AOL, then Preferences and click the WWW icon. In Advanced Settings, select Browser Display and choose an appropriate text size

Safari 1.0b

In the menu at the top of your browser window labelled 'View' there are two items labeled "Make Text Bigger" and "Make Text Smaller". Alternately, you can hold down the "command" key (the one with the apple logo on it) and press the keys '+' or '-' to increase or decrease your font size, respectively. Increasing your font size will make the text on the webpage larger (and possibly easier to read), while decreasing your font size will make the text smaller, enabling you to see more of the webpage without scrolling.

Konqueror

On the browser menu, select Settings, then Configure Konqueror. Select the Konqueror Browser icon, then click on the Appearances tab. Set the minimum and medium font sizes as appropriate.

Using another browser or experiencing problems?

If you cannot find your text size settings, please consult your browser's help file or search the web for information about text size, font size, settings, preferences or options. If you still experience problems, please contact the company who produce your browser for further advice.
