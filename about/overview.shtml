Overview: Widener Law At-A-Glance

This page provides a brief introduction to the Widener Law Experience, a snapshot of our academics & programs and an introduction to our two-campus structure. Feel free to click through using the links on this page to explore these in more detail.

The Widener University School of Law Experience

The Widener University School of Law Experience is one of scholastic excellence based upon a solid fundamental legal educations, diverse clinical involvement, and an energetic, innovative and accessible faculty. A spirit of cooperation pervades the Widener community. Widener University School of Law is committed to providing a legal education that thoroughly prepares the student to become the practicing attorney of the twenty-first century.

Academics at Widener

As a Widener student, you will receive an excellent legal education from faculty members with outstanding teaching and scholarly achievement. The basic academic programs, the legal writing and methods curriculum, the Intensive Trial Advocacy Program, numerous elective courses and substantial clinical & externships opportunities are available at both campuses. Each campus offers full-time(Full-time/Regular Division) and part-time programs (Part-time/Extended Division), allowing the Widener student greater flexibility. Both campuses have developed strong academic support programs for those students who may require additional assistance in reaching their full academic potential. Both campuses also offer a number of significant extracurricular activities for our students, and students from both campuses participate in our extraordinary Aummer Abroad Law Institutes coordinated by the International Programs Office. Widener's campuses also boast an excellent record of safety and security.

Two-Campus Structure

Widener's two-campus structure provides students with the opportunity to utilize two fully-staffed Career Development Offices. Therefore, Widener students can easily expand their job search efforts. Students at each campus have full access to all job postings, on-campus interviews and all Career Development services offered at both campuses. The two-campus structure also provides a greater alumni network. Students can enhance their job search efforts through contact with Widener graduates, regardless of campus affiliation. Widener graduates are employed throughout the country and offer the Widener student a large career networking base.
