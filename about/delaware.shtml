Wilmington, Delaware: An Ideal Place to Live and Learn

The Delaware Campus is located five miles from the heart of America's "corporate capital," Wilmington. Delaware's prestigious corporate practice environment and the national reputation of its courts provide a wealth of enriching externship and employment opportunities for Widener students. The campus is situated in the middle of the eastern metropolitan corridor near Philadelphia, Baltimore, New York and Washington, D.C., which offers students a rich diversity of employment options.

Nestled in the scenic Brandywine River Valley "chateau country," the Wilmington region boasts an impressive array of cultural attractions and exciting activities - from the internationally renowned Longwood Gardens, the museum estate of the DuPont family, and the First USA Riverfront Arts Center, to the Clifford Brown Jazz and St. Anthony's Italian Festivals.

Delaware is also home to beautiful beach resorts with exceptional shopping and some of the finest cuisine. Outdoor enthusiasts may enjoy Delaware's thousands of acres of parkland for fishing, canoeing, boating and hiking. History abounds and is fascinatingly restored at the Hagley Museum and Library and on the banks of the Brandywine, and is still alive in the colonial era buildings of Old New Castle. Nearby Philadelphia, the birthplace of our nation, offers a wealth of historical attractions, from the Liberty Bell to Independence Hall. Both Philadelphia and Baltimore provide many things to see and do, including concerts, dazzling nightlife, worldclass museums and national sports teams such as the Phillies, the Eagles, the Orioles, the Ravens, the Flyers and the 76ers. Whether you enjoy big cities or beautiful scenery, Wilmington, Delaware is an ideal place to live and learn.
