Harrisburg, Pennsylvania: A City with "Capital" Opportunities

Widener's Harrisburg Campus is located in Pennsylvania's capital city, only minutes from the Capitol Complex. The legislature, state and federal courts and governmental agencies provide a multitude of learning, externship and employment opportunities for aspiring lawyers. The region is home to many large medical and financial institutions, as well as numerous national and international corporations, including Rite Aid Corp., Tyco Inc., Hershey Foods and Harley Davidson. The robust economy of the area also provides numerous employment opportunities for our graduates.

The capital region abounds with cultural and recreational opportunities to satisfy the diverse interests of our students and to further complement their law school experiences. Lovers of the outdoors may choose to hike the Appalachian Trail, fish on renowned trout streams or stroll along the Susquehanna River. Historians will especially enjoy the National Civil War Museum in Harrisburg, which opened in 2001, and the nearby battlefields and museums of Gettysburg.

Sports enthusiasts in our student body have the opportunity to take in major league indoor soccer games, minor league baseball, hockey, outdoor soccer games and semi-professional basketball and football games. The capital region also offers art galleries, theater and the symphony. The Whitaker Center, located in downtown Harrisburg, houses a science center and IMAX theatre. Central Pennsylvania also boasts shopping opportunities, both at outlet malls and in Amish communities. Philadelphia and Washington, D.C., each only 100 miles from Harrisburg, and New York City, less than 200 miles away, provide our students with interesting day trip destinations. Whether you prefer back roads or bright lights, central Pennsylvania provides a community that is safe, comfortable and conducive to your personal, academic and professional development.

Working in Harrisburg

Situated in the heart of Pennsylvania, the capital city is an excellent place to live, to study, or to practice law. Downtown, the Capitol Complex is home to:

  • the Pennsylvania House of Representatives and Senate;
  • the Supreme, Superior, and Commonwealth Courts of Pennsylvania;
  • the Governor's Office;
  • the Attorney General; and
  • numerous state administrative agencies.

It is an excellent market for legal professionals. Because of the legal activities of government, many nationally prominent law firms have offices in Harrisburg. There are broad opportunities in every area of government law and practice as a state attorney or as a private practitioner.

Living in Harrisburg

The City of Harrisburg combines big city attractions and resources with small town comfort and charm. In the City, the recently completed and striking Whitaker Center for Science and the Arts features frequent performances by diverse artists. The State Museum and Archives are located in the Capitol Complex. There are a multitude of outdoor activities available in the Harrisburg area, including boating on the Susquehanna River, hiking the mountains surrounding the city (the Appalachian Trail is nearby), exploring Amish country, skiing and ice skating. Four major state parks are located within one hour of downtown Harrisburg. State game lands are located in the mountains within a thirty-minute drive from downtown Harrisburg. Central Pennsylvania also has some of the best trout fishing in the United States, for those who are so inclined. Located on the river directly across from the Capitol is City Island, home to the Senators, Harrisburg's triple-A baseball team, as well as many other summertime activities. Harrisburg and nearby Hershey are also home to professional sports teams in ice hockey and soccer. One of the largest and most well-known amusement parks in the country, Hershey Park, is located nearby.

The City of Harrisburg is within two hours driving distance from Philadelphia, Washington D.C., and Baltimore and three hours from New York City. The City is about one and one half hours away from the scenic Pocono Mountain resort area, one of the premier year-round vacation areas in the eastern United States, which also offers excellent skiing. Widener's location is perfect for those who seek big city excitement on the weekends or getting away to the outdoors in any season. In addition to varied activities to suit most tastes, a low cost of living - housing is relatively inexpensive - and a strong economy give students and residents of Harrisburg a distinct economic advantage.
