Law School Announces New Faculty Members

The Harrisburg Campus welcomes back Visiting Associate Professor Harry L. Witte, a former member of the full-time and adjunct faculty. Professor Witte is a long-time private practitioner in the Harrisburg area and former General Counsel of the Florida Commission on Human Relations. Professor Witte earned his J.D. and M.P.A. degrees at Harvard University. Professor Witte's teaching and writing expertise is in the area of Constitutional Law.

Mark Wilson is a Visiting Assistant Professor who also held that position at Penn State-Dickinson and clerked for Pennsylvania Supreme Court Justice J. Michael Eakin. He has an LL.M. from Yeshiva University and his J.D. from Penn State-Dickinson. Professor Wilson specializes in the field of Criminal Law.

Joining the Legal Methods faculty is Visiting Legal Methods Professor David Raeker-Jordan. He received his J.D. from the University of South Carolina and has been employed as a pro bono attorney and counselor for the homeless, a computer consultant and legal editor.

There are eight new additions to the Delaware faculty. Visiting Professor Serena Williams (who says she does not play tennis and had the name first) is a tenured professor at the University of Louisville School of Law. A 1984 graduate of Georgetown University Law Center, she received an LL.M. from George Washington University School of Law in 1992 as well. Professor Williams teaches and writes in the areas of property and environmental law. She has also been a visiting professor at the DePaul University College of Law and started her legal career as an attorney with the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development.

Associate Professor Stephen Henderson teaches and writes in the area of intellectual property and criminal law and procedure. Most recently, Professor Henderson was with the Dallas firm of Fish & Richardson and before that served as a visiting professor at Chicago-Kent College of Law. Following his graduation from Yale Law School, where he served as an articles editor on the Yale Journal on Regulation, Professor Henderson was a law clerk for Judge Jerry E. Smith of the United States Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit.

Associate Professor Nicholas Mirkay brings to Widener eleven years experience as a tax and business lawyer, most recently as a partner in the St. Louis firm of Lewis, Rice & Fingersh. His tax background also includes service in the Office of Chief Counsel of the Internal Revenue Service and a clerkship for Judge Carolyn Miller Parr on the United States Tax Court. Professor Mirkay earned his J.D. from the University of Missouri-Columbia School of Law, where he served as Associate Editor-in-Chief of the Missouri Law Review, and he added an LL.M. in taxation from the Georgetown University Law Center in 1996.

Visiting Associate Professor Jules Epstein joins Widener from the highly respected Philadelphia criminal defense firm of Kairys, Rudosky, Epstein & Messing. He will be teaching criminal law and evidence courses. A 1978 graduate of the University of Pennsylvania School of Law, Professor Epstein began his legal career with the Defender Association of Philadelphia. He has been an adjunct professor at the University of Pennsylvania since 1988, has taught in and prepared materials for countless CLE programs, and has authored dozens of articles and book chapters on criminal law and evidence topics.

Andrew J. Fichter joined our faculty in January of 2003. He brings a wealth of experience to our health law curriculum, with a specialization in the business side of health law. In addition to courses in that area, Professor Fichter will be teaching Contracts and Business Organizations. He is a 1985 graduate of Yale Law School and practiced business and health law with a number of Philadelphia law firms before joining our faculty. He has prior teaching experience as an adjunct professor at Villanova Law School and the University of the Sciences in Philadelphia. Before embarking on his legal career, Professor Fichter was an English professor at Princeton University, having earned a Ph.D. in English literature from Yale in 1974.

Three new Legal Methods Professors have also joined our faculty this year. Professor Iva Johnson Ferrell has an MBA from Rutgers University and earned her J.D. from the University of Pittsburgh in 1997. She has worked as an attorney for QVC and was an associate with the Philadelphia law firms of Morgan Lewis and Pepper Hamilton. Professor Alison Kehner was the valedictorian of her graduating class at the Villanova University School of Law where she also served as the symposium editor of the Villanova Law Review. Following law school, Professor Kehner clerked for Judge Mary Cooper of the United States District Court for the District of New Jersey, and for Judge Marjorie Rendell of the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit. She was also an associate in the Dechert firm in Philadelphia. Professor Susan King, a former law librarian at Rutgers-Camden, earned her J.D. from Temple Law School and her M.L.S. at Rutgers University. She is a former associate at Pepper Hamilton, and has authored numerous publications in library journals.



Delaware Campus, October 2003
