Dennis S. Corgill

B.A., Stanford University
M.A., University of Chicago
J.D., Yale Law School

Dennis S. Corgill is Professor of Law at Widener's Harrisburg campus. Professor Corgill received a B.A. from Stanford University in 1973; an M.A. from the University of Chicago in 1977, and a J.D. from Yale Law School in 1982.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Corgill worked in private practice and with the Antitrust Division of the United States Department of Justice.

Professor Corgill was on the faculty of the University of Missouri, Kansas City, from 1986-92, and then joined the faculty at Widener in 1992. Professor Corgill is admitted to practice in California and the District of Columbia, and he teaches and writes in the areas of Antitrust, Law and Economics, Business Organizations, Contracts, and Intellectual Property.

Professor Corgill is an active member of a number of professional organizations, including the State Bar of California, The District of Columbia Bar, the American Bar Association, the American Law and Economics Association, and the American Intellectual Property Law Association.

Professor Corgill is on Leave of Absence from the Law School for academic year 2003-04 and is teaching at Santa Clara University School of Law.
