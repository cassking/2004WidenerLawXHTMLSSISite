Loren D. Prescott, Jr.

B.A., University of Washington
J.D., Willamette University College of Law
LL.M., University of Florida College of Law

Loren D. Prescott, Jr. is Professor of Law at Widener's Harrisburg campus. Professor Prescott received his B.A. from the University of Washington, Seattle, in 1979, a J.D. from Willamette University College of Law in 1984, and an LL.M. in Taxation from the University of Florida College of Law in 1989.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Prescott practiced with Bullivant, Houser, Bailey in Portland, Oregon, from 1984 to 1986, and with LeGros, Buchanan, Paul, Prescott & Whitehead in Seattle, Washington, from 1986 to 1987. He served as Vice President and General Counsel to Wy'east Color, Inc., Bellevue, Washington, from 1987 to 1988.

Professor Prescott joined the faculty at the University of Florida College of Law as a Visiting Assistant Professor in 1989. He came to Widener as an Assistant Professor in 1990, served as Dean of Students 1992-94 and as Associate Professor from 1993-2002, when he was promoted to Full Professor. He served as the Vice Dean for Widener's Harrisburg campus from 1996 to 2000.

Professor Prescott is admitted to practice in Oregon and Washington, and he teaches and writes in the areas of Federal Income Taxation and Charitable Organizations.

Professor Prescott is an active member of a number of professional and civic organizations, including the Dauphin County Bar Association, the Pennsylvania Bar Association and the Cumberland County Inn of Court. He currently serves as president of the St. Thomas More Society of Central Pennsylvania and is a board member of Real Alternatives, Inc. and Lawyers Concerned for Lawyers. Professor Prescott also serves as an Advocate in the Office of the Tribunal for the Diocese of Harrisburg and as a volunteer at the Harrisburg State Hospital.
