Anthony J. Fejfar

B.A., Creighton University
J.D., University of Nebraska College of Law

Anthony J. Fejfar is Associate Professor of Law at Widener's Harrisburg campus. Professor Fejfar received a B.A., magna cum laude, from Creighton University in 1981, and a J.D., with Distinction, from the University of Nebraska College of Law in 1985, where he served as a member of the Nebraska Law Review and was a member of Order of the Coif.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Fejfar served as a Visiting Assistant Professor of Law at Marquette from 1988-89, and joined the faculty at Widener in 1989 as Assistant Professor of Law. He was Assistant Professor of Law from 1989-92, and has been Associate Professor of Law since 1992.

Professor Fejfar is admitted to practice in Nebraska and Pennsylvania; he teaches and writes in the areas of Property, Interviewing and Counseling, Professional Responsibility, Alternate Dispute Resolution, and Land Use Planning.

Professor Fejfar has been active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including the Lower Paxton Area Jaycees and Neighborhood Dispute Resolution of Dauphin County. He presently serves on the Pennsylvania Bar Association Task Force on Quality of Life and Balance.
