Serena M. Williams

A.B., Smith College
J.D., Georgetown University Law Center
LL.M., George Washington University National Law Center

Serena M. Williams is Visiting Professor of Law at Widener's Delaware campus. Professor Williams received an A.B. in Economics from Smith College in 1981, where she was President of the Student Government Association and served on the Board of Trustees for the college. Professor Williams received her J.D. from Georgetown University Law Center in 1984 and her LL.M. from George Washington University National Law Center in 1992.

Professor Williams worked as a Financial Economist at the Office of Economic and Tax Policy, District of Columbia Department of Finance and Revenue in Washington, D.C. from 1989-1990; as an attorney at the Office of Program Enforcement of the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development in Washington, D.C. from 1984-1987; and clerked for the National Association of Home Builders in Washington, D.C. from 1983-1984.

Professor Williams is admitted to practice in Maryland and the District of Columbia.

Professor Williams has been active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including the Kentucky Environmental Quality Commission where she served as Commissioner until December 2003; on the Board of Directors of ACLU of Kentucky; the Alumnae Association of Smith College Board of Directors Nominating Committee and the National Alumnae Admissions Committee; the First Gethsemane Center for Family Development, Inc. of Louisville, Kentucky, and Planned Parenthood of Louisville Board of Directors.
