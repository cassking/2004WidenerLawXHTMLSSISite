Alison Donahue Kehner

B.A., University of Delaware
J.D., Villanova University School of Law

Alison Donahue Kehner is a Legal Writing Professor at Widener's Delaware campus. Professor Kehner received a B.A., magna cum laude, in History and Criminal Justice from the University of Delaware in 1994 and a J.D., summa cum laude, from Villanova University School of Law in 1997 where she was a member of the Villanova Law Review.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Kehner clerked for the Honorable Mary L. Cooper, United States District Court, District of New Jersey from 1997-1999. Professor Kehner served as appellate law clerk to the Honorable Marjorie O. Rendell in the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit from 1999-2001. She was employed as an associate attorney in the litigation department at Dechert in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, from 2001-2003.

Professor Kehner is admitted to practice in Pennsylvania and New Jersey.
