Robert L. Hayman, Jr.

B.A., Davidson College
J.D., Georgetown University Law Center
LL.M., Temple University School of Law

Robert L. Hayman, Jr., is Professor of Law at Widener's Delaware campus, and the H. Albert Young Fellow in Constitutional Law for 2003-2005. Professor Hayman received a B.A. from Davidson College in 1978, a J.D. from Georgetown University Law Center in 1981, and an LL.M. from Temple University School of Law in 1990.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Hayman served as Staff Attorney, Legal Aid of Western Missouri, Kansas City, from 1981-82 and Assistant Director and Clinical Instructor in the DC Street Law Project from 1982-85. He was also a Lecturer at the University of Missouri, Kansas City from 1987-90.

Professor Hayman joined the faculty at Widener in 1990 as Assistant Professor of Law. He was then Associate Professor of Law from 1993-1999, when he became Professor of Law. Professor Hayman is admitted to practice in Missouri, and he teaches and writes in the areas of Constitutional Law, Civil Rights, Equal Protection Law, and Jurisprudence.
