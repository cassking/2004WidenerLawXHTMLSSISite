Andrew L. Strauss

A.B., Princeton University
J.D., New York University School of Law

Andrew Strauss is a professor of international law at Widener University School of Law. He earned his Bachelor of Arts at Princeton University's Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs and his Juris Doctorate at New York University School of Law.

In the early 1980s, Professor Strauss served as a Fulbright Fellow in Ecuador, and in the mid 1980s, he taught on the Law Faculty at the National University of Singapore.

After returning to the United States, Professor Strauss practiced international corporate and finance law in New York for the law firms of Shearman & Sterling and Graham & James.

Professor Strauss has been active in many civic and professional organizations. He has conducted human rights missions and been a consultant to both Human Rights Watch and the Lawyers Committee for Human Rights. Professor Strauss is a member of the Executive Committee of the Section on International Law of the Association of American Law Schools, is on the Editorial Board of World Editorial and International Law, and has both taught in and served as a director of the Geneva International Law Institute and the Nairobi International Law Institute. He has lectured widely around the world.

Professor Strauss' primary areas of research and writing are global democratization, international environmental law, international human rights and international jurisdiction. His articles have appeared in such professional and popular publications as the Harvard and Stanford journals of international law, Foreign Affairs Magazine, the International Herald Tribune and the Financial Times. His proposal for litigating global warming was selected last year by the New York Times Magazine as one of the most innovative ideas of the year.
