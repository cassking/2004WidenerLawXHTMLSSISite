Leonard N. Sosnov

B.A., Temple University
J.D., Harvard University Law School

Leonard N. Sosnov is Professor of Law at Widener's Delaware campus. Professor Sosnov received a B.A. from Temple University in 1967, and a J.D. from Harvard University Law School in 1971.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Sosnov clerked for the Honorable Judge Joseph S. Lord, III, U.S.D.C., Eastern District of Pennsylvania, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, from 1971-72; Staff Attorney, Defender Association of Philadelphia, from 1972-90.

Professor Sosnov joined the faculty at Widener as Associate Professor of Law and has served in that capacity since 1990. Professor Sosnov is admitted to practice in Pennsylvania, and he teaches and writes in the areas of Criminal Law, Criminal Procedure, and Evidence.

Professor Sosnov was a Visiting Associate Professor of Law at Temple University School of Law for the Fall 2000 semester. He has represented individuals on behalf of Centurion Ministries, a non-profit organization dedicated to freeing innocent individuals sentenced to life imprisonment or death.
