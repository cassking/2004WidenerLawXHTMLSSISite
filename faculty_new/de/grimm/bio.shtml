Eugene L. Grimm

B.S.L., University of Minnesota
J.D., University of Minnesota
LL.M., George Washington University National Law Center

Eugene L. Grimm is Visiting Professor of Law at Widener's Delaware campus. Professor Grimm received his B.S.L. and J.D. degrees from the University of Minnesota (J.D. 1950), and his LL.M. degree from the George Washington University National Law Center in 1958.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Grimm served as 1st Lt., USA JAGC from 1950-52; Commissioner, United States Court of Military Appeals, 1953-56; Appellate Attorney, United States Department of Justice, 1957-1960; General Counsel, Administrative Office, United States Courts, 1960-62; Corporate Counsel, E.I. du Pont de Nemours & Company, 1962-89; Visiting Professor of Law, Widener University School of Law, Delaware campus, 1990-96, 1998-present.

Professor Grimm is admitted to practice before the Supreme Court of the United States and in the states of Delaware and Minnesota. Professor Grimm is a charter member of the Benjamin Franklin American Inn of Court for Intellectual Property, a member of the Delaware State Bar Association, and a member of various local professional and charitable organizations.

Professor Grimm teaches in the areas of Trademarks and Unfair Trade Practices, Antitrust, and Health Care Antitrust.
