Robert Justin Lipkin

B.A., Brooklyn College
M.A., Princeton University
Ph.D., Princeton University
J.D., University of California at Los Angeles School of Law

Robert Justin Lipkin is Professor of Law at Widener's Delaware Campus. From July 1999 to June 2001, Professor Lipkin was the first H. Albert Young Fellow in Constitutional Law at Widener University School of Law.

Professor Lipkin received a B.A. from Brooklyn College in 1965; an M.A. from Princeton University in 1971; a Ph.D. from Princeton University in 1974; and a J.D. from University of California at Los Angeles School of Law in 1984, where he served as Associate Editor of the U.C.L.A. Law Review.

Following graduation from law school, he clerked for the Honorable Gilbert S. Merritt, United States Court of Appeals, 6th Circuit, from 1984-85. He then joined the faculty at Delaware Law School, where he teaches Constitutional Law and Theory, and a variety of seminars in Jurisprudence, Constitutionalism, Democracy and Multiculturalism.

Professor Lipkin is admitted to practice in California. Professor Lipkin has been a consultant for the American Civil Liberties Foundation and for Planned Parenthood of Lincoln, Nebraska. He is a member of the American Philosophical Association, the American Political Science Association, and the Society for Political and Legal Philosophy.
