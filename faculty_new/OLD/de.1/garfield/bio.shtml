Alan E. Garfield

B.A., Brandeis University
J.D., University of California at Los Angeles School of Law

Alan E. Garfield is a Professor of Law at Widener's Delaware campus. He received his B.A. in 1979 from Brandeis University, where he graduated magna cum laude, and his J.D. in 1983 from UCLA School of Law, where he was a member of the Order of the Coif and the UCLA Law Review.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Garfield worked for three years in the litigation department of Weil, Gotshal & Manges in New York City.

Professor Garfield joined the Widener faculty in 1986. He was promoted to Associate Professor in 1989 and Full Professor in 1999. He is admitted to practice in California and New York. He teaches and writes in the areas of First Amendment, Copyright and Contracts, including recent publications in the Cornell Law Review, Georgia Law Review and Temple Law Review.

Professor Garfield has been active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including the California, Delaware, and New York State Bar Associations. His recent activities include: presenting a paper on "Copyright in the Digital Age" at a University of Delaware Linux Users' Group meeting; presenting a paper on "Copyright and Speech" at the Philadelphia Intellectual Property Law Association's annual spring meeting; presenting a paper on "The First Amendment as a Check on Copyright Rights" at the 13th Annual Computer Law Symposium: "Beta Testing the First Amendment: Balancing Intellectual Property and Freedom in a Networked Society" at Hastings College of Law in San Francisco; and presenting a paper on "Delaware and European Corporate Law: A Comparative Analysis" at a Delaware State Bar Continuing Legal Education program.
