James R. May

B.S., University of Kansas
J.D., University of Kansas School of Law
LL.M., Pace University School of Law

James R. May is a tenured Associate Professor of Law; Director, Environmental and Natural Resources Law Clinic; Director, Sydney International Law Institute and Adjunct Professor of Graduate Engineering at Widener's Delaware campus.

Professor May received a B.S. from University of Kansas in 1985; a J.D. from University of Kansas School of Law in 1989; and, an LL.M. in Environmental Law from Pace University School of Law in 1991.

Following graduation from law school, Professor May served as Environmental Attorney, Black & Veatch, Overland Park, Kansas, from 1989-90 and Environmental Fellow, Pace Center for Environmental Legal Studies, 1990-91.

Professor May joined the faculty at Widener in 1991 as Assistant Professor of Law, and served in that capacity until 1994; he has served as Associate Professor of Law since 1994. Professor May has also taught in the Graduate Engineering Program since 1992. Professor May is admitted to practice in Kansas and Pennsylvania.

Professor May teaches and writes in the areas of: Environmental Law, Environmental Law for Engineers, Natural Resources Law and International Environmental Law.
