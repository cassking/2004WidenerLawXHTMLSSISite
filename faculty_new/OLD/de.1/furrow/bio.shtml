Barry R. Furrow

B.A., Harvard College
J.D., Harvard Law School

Barry Ray Furrow is Professor of Law and Director of the Health Law Institute at the Widener University School of Law. He received his J.D. in 1971 from Harvard Law School and his undergraduate degree in 1967 from Harvard College.

Before going into teaching, Professor Furrow clerked with the Superior Court of Massachusetts and then practiced law with Palmer & Dodge in Boston, Massachusetts. He has taught as a regular or visiting faculty member at the law schools of the University of Michigan, the University of Detroit, American University, the University of North Carolina, and George Mason University.

Professor Furrow is a member of the editorial board of the Journal of Law, Medicine & Ethics. He is a regular peer reviewer for the Journal of the American Medical Association and the Journal of Health Policy, Politics and Law, and has published more than three dozen articles in such journals as the University of Pennsylvania Law Review, the Harvard Civil Rights-Civil Liberties Law Review, the St. Louis University Law Journal, the Boston University Law Review, and Health Matrix.

Professor Furrow has been active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including the American Civil Liberties Union (Delaware Chapter), the American Society of Law, Medicine and Ethics, and the Society of Healthcare Attorneys of Pennsylvania.
