Douglas E. Ray

University of Minnesota
J.D., Harvard Law School

Douglas E. Ray is Dean of Widener University School of Law, Vice-President for the Law Center, and Professor of Law. Before joining Widener University School of Law in January, 1999, Dean Ray taught at the University of Toledo Law School where he served as Charles W. Fornoff Professor of Law and Values and also as an associate dean.

A graduate of the University of Minnesota and the Harvard Law School, Dean Ray has been an economist with the U.S. Department of Labor, a labor attorney with Dorsey & Whitney in Minneapolis and a professor at the University of Richmond Law School. He served in the United States Army from 1966 to 1970.

Dean Ray has been active in a number of professional and civic organizations including state and local bar associations and the Association of American Law Schools.

In 1995, he served as chair of the Labor and Employment Law Section of the Association of American Law Schools and was named to the National Academy of Arbitrators.

Dean Ray has also been a frequent speaker on national programs concerning labor and employment law. In 1998, for example, he spoke in Chicago, Illinois, on labor arbitration advocacy at the Ninth Annual Labor-Management Conference sponsored by the Federal Mediation and Conciliation Service. He also spoke on the role of labor arbitration in labor relations at the Bernard Gottfried Memorial Labor Law Symposium held in Detroit, Michigan, and sponsored by the National Labor Relations Board and the Wayne State University Law School.

Since 1999, he has chaired the annual four-day Short Course in Labor Law and Labor Arbitration sponsored by the Southwestern Legal Foundation (now the Center for American and International Law) and held in Dallas, Texas.

Dean Ray has taught and published in the fields of labor law, employment discrimination law, torts and labor arbitration.
