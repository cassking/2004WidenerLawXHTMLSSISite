Martin A. Kotler

B.A., George Washington University
J.D., Hastings College of Law
LL.M., New York University School of Law
J.S.D., New York University School of Law

Martin A. Kotler is Professor of Law at Widener's Delaware campus. Professor Kotler received a B.A. from George Washington University in 1972, and a J.D. from Hastings College of the Law in 1975; an LL.M. from New York University School of Law in 1984, and a J.S.D. from New York University School of Law in 1989.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Kotler served as Associate Attorney, Popelka, Allard, McCowan & Jones, San Jose, California, from 1976-80; Partner, Kotler & Kotler, Walnut Creek, California, from 1980-83; Visiting Fellow, National University of Singapore, from 1985-87.

Professor Kotler joined the faculty at Delaware Law School in 1987 and served as Visiting Associate Professor of Law from 1987-88; Associate Professor of Law from 1988-93 (Widener); and as Professor of Law since 1993. Professor Kotler served as Visiting Professor of Law at Seton Hall during the Fall 1994 semester, at Santa Clara in 1996-97, and at Cal Western during the Spring 2000 semester.

Professor Kotler is admitted to practice in California, and he teaches and writes in the areas of Torts, Products Liability, Insurance Law, Contemporary Tort Theory, and Remedies.

Professor Kotler has been active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including the American Law Institute, where he has served on the members' consultative committees for the drafting of the Restatement (Third) of Torts: Products Liability and the Restatement (Third) of Torts: Apportionment of Liability Projects and the Restatement (Third) of Torts: General Principles.
