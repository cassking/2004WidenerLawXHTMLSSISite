John G. Culhane

B.A., The College of William and Mary
J.D., Fordham University School of Law

John G. Culhane is Professor of Law at Widener's Delaware campus. He also holds the title of Lecturer at the Yale University School of Public Health, where he teaches Public Health Law. Professor Culhane received a B.A. from The College of William and Mary in 1978, and a J.D. from Fordham University School of Law in 1982, where he was Associate Editor of the Fordham Law Review.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Culhane clerked for Honorable Joseph M. McLaughlin, Eastern District of New York, Brooklyn, from 1982-83. He then served as Associate Attorney for Cahill, Gordon & Reindel, New York City, from 1983-86; Bigelow Teaching Fellow and Lecturer and Instructor at the University of Chicago from 1986-87.

Professor Culhane joined the faculty at Delaware Law School in 1987 and served as Assistant Professor from 1987-91, Associate Professor of Law from 1991-96, and Professor since 1996. He was Associate Director of Graduate Programs in 1996; and Director of Corporate LL.M. Program and International Programs from 1996 through June, 1999.

Professor Culhane is admitted to practice in New York, and teaches and writes in the areas of Torts, Public Health Law, Advanced Torts, HIV/AIDS and the Law, and Family Law, particularly as it relates to the rights of same-sex couples. He has twice received the Outstanding Faculty Award, most recently in May, 2000.
