Eileen B. Cooper

B.S., Rhode Island College
M.S.L.S., Villanova University
J.D., Widener University School of Law

Eileen Cooper is Director of the Legal Information Center, Wilmington and Harrisburg campuses and Professor of Law. She earned her undergraduate degree at Rhode Island College and a Masters Degree in Library Science from Villanova University in 1973. Professor Cooper graduated cum laude from Widener University School of Law in 1976 where she was a member of the Delaware Journal of Corporate Law.

Upon graduating from law school Professor Cooper was appointed Assistant Law Librarian at Widener. In 1986, she was named Director of the Law Library. Under her leadership, the law library transitioned into the Legal Information Center, representing the integration of the law school's two campus libraries utilizing the latest resources in information technology.

Professor Cooper has served as a library consultant, law school inspector for the American Bar Association, President of the Greater Philadelphia Law Libraries Association and is a member of many professional organizations relating to library service and the law. She was a recipient of the Outstanding Service Award of the Widener Alumni Association in 1996.

Professor Cooper is admitted to practice in Pennsylvania.

In celebration of National Library Week 1997 she stated, "research capabilities are far more powerful as a result of technology. However, these advances do not match the intellectual odyssey offered by the book... There is something to be cherished about that old technology -- the book."
