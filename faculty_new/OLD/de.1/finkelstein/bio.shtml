Arlene Rivera Finkelstein

B.S., Cornell University
J.D., Temple University School of Law

Arlene Rivera Finkelstein is a Legal Writing Professor at Widener's Delaware campus. Professor Rivera Finkelstein received a B.S. from Cornell University, College of Human Ecology, in 1992, and a J.D. from Temple University School of Law in 1995.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Rivera Finkelstein worked as an Assistant Public Defender at the Defender Association of Philadelphia from 1995-2000. Subsequently, Professor Rivera Finkelstein developed a private practice specializing in the areas of criminal defense, workers' compensation, and family law.

Professor Rivera Finkelstein joined the faculty at Widener in July 2002 and teaches Legal Methods. She is admitted to practice in Pennsylvania.

Professor Rivera Finkelstein is active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including The Hispanic Bar Association of Pennsylvania, of which she is President; the Board of Directors of Community Legal Services of Philadelphia, of which she is a member; and the Philadelphia Volunteers for the Indigent Program, for which she continues to offer pro bono client representation.
