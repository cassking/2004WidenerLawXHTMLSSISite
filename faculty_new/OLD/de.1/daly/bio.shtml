Erin Daly

B.A., Wesleyan University
J.D., University of Michigan

Erin Daly is Professor of Law at Widener's Delaware campus. Professor Daly received a B.A. from Wesleyan University in 1983, and a J.D. from University of Michigan in 1989.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Daly practiced First Amendment and Employment Law at Cooper, White & Cooper, San Francisco, from 1990-93.

Professor Daly joined the faculty at Widener in 1993, serving as Assistant Professor from 1993-96 and Associate Professor from 1996-2003.

Professor Daly is admitted to practice in California, and she teaches and writes in the areas of Constitutional Law, Comparative Law, Federal Courts, Administrative Law, Civil Liberties, and First Amendment. She has recently been working on transitional justice issues throughout the world.

Professor Daly chaired a silent symposium on the independent counsel law. She has taught in and directed Widener's International Program in Sydney, Australia. She also served as a Vice-chair of the Young Lawyer's Division Law and Media Committee of the American Bar Association, which sponsors an annual media advocacy workshop.
