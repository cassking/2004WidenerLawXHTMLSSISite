Alicia Brokars Kelly

B.A., Temple University
J.D., Temple University
LL.M., Legal Education, Temple University

Alicia Kelly is an Associate Professor of Law at Widener's Delaware campus. Professor Kelly received a B.A., magna cum laude, from Temple University (Honors Program); her J.D., cum laude, and LL.M. in Legal Education, from Temple University School of Law. In recognition of academic excellence, Professor Kelly was awarded a full scholarship for her undergraduate education and a Horace Brown Scholarship during her J.D. program.

Before her law teaching career, Professor Kelly was in private practice for a total of five years, first in Pennsylvania and then in Colorado, concentrating on complex domestic relations and general civil litigation. She is also a trained mediator. Additionally, during law school Professor Kelly was a law clerk for the Honorable Judge William F. Hall in the Federal District Court, 3rd Circuit. Professor Kelly is admitted to practice in Pennsylvania and Colorado.

Professor Kelly joined the faculty at Widener in 2001 as an Associate Professor of Law. Prior to accepting her appointment at Widener, Professor Kelly was an Assistant Professor of Law on the faculty at Western State University College of Law in Orange County, California. Before that, Professor Kelly taught law full-time at Temple University School of Law in Philadelphia as an Abraham L. Freedman Fellow, while earning her LL.M. in legal education.

Professor Kelly's teaching areas are Property, Family Law, Federal Taxation, Trusts & Estates and Professional Responsibility. Her legal scholarship encompasses the areas of property, family law and professional ethics.
