Catherine J. Wasson

B.A., Kent State University
M.A., Kent State University
J.D., William Mitchell College of Law

Catherine J. Wasson is an Associate Professor of Law and Director of the Legal Methods Program on the Harrisburg campus. Professor Wasson received B.A. and M.A. degrees from Kent State University, and received her J.D. from William Mitchell College of Law, St. Paul, Minnesota, in 1988. While in law school, Professor Wasson took first-place honors in both the appellate advocacy and advanced trial advocacy competitions, served as director of the Moot Court Board, and was awarded the Alumni/ae Association Student Award of Merit.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Wasson held a clerkship with the Minnesota Court of Appeals, and later was on the staff of the Minnesota Workers' Compensation Court of Appeals. She was a member of the adjunct faculty at William Mitchell College of Law from 1992-98, where she taught Legal Writing & Research, worked with the law school's upperclass Practicum, and provided academic support services.

Since joining the Widener faculty in 1998, Professor Wasson has taught Legal Methods, Family Law, Torts, and Intensive Legal Analysis. She has been Director of the Legal Methods Program since 2001.

Professor Wasson has been active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including the Public Law Section of the Minnesota State Bar Association, the Association of Legal Writing Directors, and the Legal Writing Institute. She has served as a volunteer guardian ad litem in Hennepin County, Minnesota, and performed pro bono work for the Legal Aid Society of Minneapolis. She was a Qualified Neutral under Rule 114, Minnesota General Rules of Practice, certified to perform both civic and family mediation.

Professor Wasson has co-authored a law review article and several continuing education manuals in the area of workers' compensation. She has also served as an editor and reviewer for the Journal of Law and Social Work, and was a presenter of a major workshop at the 1999 Institute for Law School Teaching.
