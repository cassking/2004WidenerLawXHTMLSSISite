Mark D. Wilson

B.A., Rutgers University
J.D., Pennsylvania State University Dickinson School of Law
LL.M., Yeshiva University

Mark Wilson is a Visiting Assistant Professor on the Harrisburg campus. Professor Wilson received his B.A. from Rutgers University in 1993; his J.D. from the Pennsylvania State University Dickinson School of Law in 2001, and his LL.M. from Yeshiva University in 2002.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Wilson clerked for Justice J. Michael Eakin of the Supreme Court of Pennsylvania.

Professor Wilson was a Visiting Assistant Professor at The Dickinson School of Law, where he taught Criminal Procedure, Advanced Criminal Procedure and Lawyering Skills. He has also worked as a Trooper on the Wyoming State Highway Patrol, where he was a Special Services Squad Member from 1994-97. Professor Wilson was an Infantry Paratrooper with the United States Army from 1986-90.
