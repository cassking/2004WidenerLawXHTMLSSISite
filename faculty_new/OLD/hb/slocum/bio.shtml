Brian G. Slocum

B.B.A., Pacific Union College
J.D., Harvard Law School

Brian G. Slocum is a Visiting Associate Professor of Law at Widener's Harrisburg campus. Professor Slocum received a B.B.A. in Accounting from Pacific Union College in 1996, and a J.D. from Harvard Law School in 1999.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Slocum clerked for the Honorable Frank Magill, United States Court of Appeals for the Eighth Circuit, from 1999-2000. He then worked for the United States Department of Justice in both the Civil and Criminal Divisions.

Professor Slocum joins Widener University School of Law in Spring 2004 as a Visiting Associate Professor of Law, where he will teach Contracts and Immigration Law. Professor Slocum has taught as an adjunct professor at The George Washington University Law School. His courses included Legal Research and Writing and Introduction to Advocacy.

Professor Slocum is admitted to practice in the State of Maryland.
