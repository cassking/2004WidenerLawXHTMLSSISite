Anna P. Hemingway

B.S., Drexel University
M.A., West Chester University
J.D., Temple University

Anna Hemingway is a Legal Methods Professor and Director of Academic Support at Widener's Harrisburg campus. Professor Hemingway received a B.S. from Drexel University in 1988, an M.A. (Clinical Psychology) from West Chester University in 1990, and a J.D. from Temple University School of Law in 1994.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Hemingway served as Assistant Corporate Counsel for the Parmer Corporations in Harrisburg, Pennsylvania, from 1995-1998. Professor Hemingway also served as a Business Law Instructor/Adjunct Professor at the Lancaster Campus of Harrisburg Area Community College from 1997-1998.

Professor Hemingway joined the faculty at Widener in August, 1998. She is admitted to practice law in Pennsylvania and New Jersey, and she teaches and writes in the areas of Intensive Legal Analysis, Legal Methods, Interviewing and Counseling and Legal Ethics.

Professor Hemingway has been active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including the Pennsylvania Bar Association, the American Bar Association, and the Legal Writing Institute. She also serves as an Assistant Editor for The Journal of the Legal Writing Institute.
