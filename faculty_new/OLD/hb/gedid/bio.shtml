John L. Gedid

B.A., University of Pittsburgh
J.D., Duquesne University School of Law
LL.M., Yale Law School

John L. Gedid is Professor of Law and Director of the Law & Government Institute at Widener's Harrisburg campus. Professor Gedid received a B.A., summa cum laude, from the University of Pittsburgh in 1963, where he was also elected to membership in Phi Beta Kappa; a J.D. from Duquesne University School of Law in 1967, where he served as Comment Editor of the Duquesne Law Review; and an LL.M. from Yale Law School in 1976.

After graduation from law school, Professor Gedid clerked for the United States District Court, Western District of Pennsylvania, from 1967-68; Associate in Private Practice from 1968-72; Assistant Professor of Law, Duquesne, from 1972-74; Associate Professor of Law, Duquesne, from 1974-77; and as Professor of Law, Duquesne, from 1977-89. Professor Gedid was Acting Dean at Duquesne in 1982.

Professor Gedid joined the faculty at Widener in 1988 as Professor of Law and served as Associate Dean for the Harrisburg campus from 1989-95. Professor Gedid is admitted to practice in Pennsylvania, and he teaches and writes in the areas of Federal and State Administrative Law, Contracts, Legislation, Sales & Leases, and Conflict of Laws.

Professor Gedid is an elected member of The American Law Institute. He is the Official Reporter of Decisions for The Commonwealth Court of Pennsylvania, and has served an extended term on the Civil Procedural Rules Committee of The Supreme Court of Pennsylvania, where he chaired several major subcommittees. He is a Pennsylvania Commissioner to the National Conference of Commissioners on Uniform State Laws (NCCUSL). In 2003, NCCUSL appointed him Reporter for revision of the Model State Administrative Procedure Act of 1981. In that capacity, he will coordinate the revision of this national statute working with a group of experts in administrative law.

Professor Gedid is active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including the American Law Institute, American Bar Association, Pennsylvania Bar Association, Dauphin County Bar Association and Allegheny County Bar Association; Catholic Charities of the Diocese of Pittsburgh; the Capitol Preservation Commission and United Way of Harrisburg.

Professor Gedid is past chair of the Administrative Law Section of the Pennsylvania Bar Association and also sits on the Council, the governing board, of that section. Professor Gedid is also editor of the PBA Administrative Law Journal. He lectures regularly on legal subjects for the Pennsylvania Bar Institute. He served as co-chair of the ABA Committee on Jurisdiction in Cyberspace involving Sale of Goods. He has served as the president of the Board of Directors of Catholic Charities of the Diocese of Harrisburg and of the Diocese of Pittsburgh. He is a Master of the Bowman Inn of Court in Harrisburg and has served as a consultant to the Supreme Court of Pennsylvania and to the Office of General Counsel of the Governor.
