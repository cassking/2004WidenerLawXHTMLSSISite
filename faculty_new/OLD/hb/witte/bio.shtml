Harry L. Witte

A.B., Spring Hill College
M.P.A., John F. Kennedy School of Government
J.D., Harvard Law School

Harry L. Witte is a Visiting Associate Professor on the Harrisburg Campus. The Law School welcomes Professor Witte back to the Harrisburg campus where he was a former member of the full-time and adjunct faculty.

Professor Witte received an A.B., magna cum laude, from Spring Hill College in 1966, a Master's in Public Administration from the John F. Kennedy School of Government in 1970 and a J.D. from Harvard Law School in 1970, where he was a HUD Urban Public Service Fellow from 1969-1970.

Professor Witte is admitted to practice in Massachusetts, New York, Florida, Michigan, and Pennsylvania.

Professor Witte is a long-time private practitioner in the Harrisburg area. He was a Senior Attorney at MidPenn Legal Services, where his practice focused on family and disability law.

Professor Witte was in private practice in Tallahassee, Florida, from 1980-1990, when he joined the Widener University School of Law faculty in Harrisburg as Associate Professor Law. Professor Witte taught Constitutional Law, State Constitutional Law, Human Rights in a Federal System, Family and Alternative Dispute Resolution in Family Law from 1990-1997.

Professor Witte was General Counsel of the Florida Commission on Human Relations from 1978-80.
