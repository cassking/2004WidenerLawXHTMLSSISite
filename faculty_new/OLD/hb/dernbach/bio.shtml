John C. Dernbach

B.S., University of Wisconsin, Eau Claire
J.D., University of Michigan

John C. Dernbach is Professor of Law at Widener's Harrisburg campus. Professor Dernbach received a B.S. from University of Wisconsin-Eau Claire in 1975, and a J.D. from the University of Michigan in 1978, where he served as Legal Notes Editor for the Michigan Journal of Legal Reform.

Following graduation from law school, Professor Dernbach worked as Legal Writing Instructor at Wayne State University from 1978-79; Staff Attorney, American Lung Association of Michigan, Lansing, 1979-80; Assistant Counsel, Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Resources, Harrisburg, Pennsylvania, 1981-87; Special Assistant, Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Resources, 1987-92 and Director of Advanced Science and Research Team, Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Resources, 1992-93.

Professor Dernbach joined the faculty at Widener as a Visiting Associate Professor of Law in 1993. He served in that role from 1993-94 until 2001, when he was promoted to full professor.

Professor Dernbach is admitted to practice in Michigan and Pennsylvania. Professor Dernbach teaches and writes in the areas of Administrative Law, Environmental Law, International Law, International Environmental Law, Property, Sustainability and the Law, and Climate Change.

Professor Dernbach has been active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including the Susquehanna Conference, the American Bar Association's Central and East European Law Initiative, and the President's Council on Sustainable Development. He is also a member of the executive committee for the Pennsylvania Resources Council and Pennsylvania Consortium for Interdisciplinary Environmental Policy.

Professor Dernbach has spoken to state, national, and international audiences on environmental issues and sustainable development. He edited a book on U.S. efforts to achieve sustainable development in the first ten years following the 1992 United Nations Conference on Environment and Development (or Earth Summit). Stumbling Toward Sustainability was recently published by the Environmental Law Institute in 2002.

Professor Dernbach is on a two-year leave of absence from the Law School to direct the Office of Policy at the Pennsylvania Department of Environmental Protection in Harrisburg, Pennsylvania.
