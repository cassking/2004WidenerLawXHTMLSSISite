John J. Capowski

Hamilton College
J.D., Cornell Law School

John J. Capowski is an Associate Professor of Law at Widener's Harrisburg campus. He is an honors graduate of Hamilton College and obtained his Doctor of Law from the Cornell Law School.

Following law school graduation, Professor Capowski worked as an attorney with the Monroe County Legal Assistance Corporation in Rochester, New York. Following his work with that federally funded Legal Services program, he returned to Cornell as a faculty member and served as the Director of Cornell's clinical program.

Professor Capowski then joined the faculty of the University of Maryland School of Law, where he served as an associate professor and directed the Legal Services Clinic, one of Maryland's clinical programs. He also has been the Director of Education and Training for the Maryland Office of the Attorney General and served as a visiting faculty member at both the University of New Mexico and West Virginia University.

In 1994, Professor Capowski joined Widener's faculty as an associate professor. He has written in the areas of administrative law, evidence, lawyering skills, public benefits, public interest law, and fly fishing. He teaches evidence, civil procedure, comparative law, and a seminar focused on public interest legislation. He has served as Director of Clinical Programs (Harrisburg), Director of the Harrisburg externship program, and Director of the Harrisburg campus' Intensive Trial Advocacy Program.

Professor Capowski has been active in a number of professional and civic organizations, including the Pennsylvania Coalition Against Domestic Violence, the Children's Rights Committee of the Pennsylvania Bar Association, and the American Bar Association's Project on Educating to End Domestic Violence: Law School Programs as Resources Against Domestic Violence.
