Most students enroll in the J.D. Regular Division program spanningthre e academic years and six semesters. Widener University School of Law awards the Juris Doctor degree to students who successfully complete a minimum of 87 credits of prescribed and elective course- work taken during the six-semester program. Our enrollment options include:

  • J.D. Full-Time
  • J.D. Part-Time
  • J.D. Midyear Admissions (MAP)
  • J.D. Trial Admissions (TAP)
  • J.D. PA State Partnership (Harrisburg)
  • J.D. Dual-Degrees Program:
  • J.D./M.S.L.S. in Law and library Science with Clarion University
